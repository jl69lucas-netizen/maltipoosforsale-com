Section 6: Maltipoo Breed Information – Everything You Need to Know

What Makes Maltipoos the Perfect Family Dog?

The Maltipoo is a designer crossbreed between a purebred Maltese and a purebred Poodle (Toy or Miniature). This intentional hybrid combines the best traits of both parent breeds, creating an affectionate, intelligent, low-shedding companion perfect for families, seniors, and allergy sufferers.

Learn more about designer breeds: American Kennel Club – Designer Dogs

Temperament & Personality: The Maltipoo Character

Core Personality Traits:

  • Affectionate: Maltipoos are “velcro dogs” who bond deeply with their families and thrive on human companionship
  • Playful: Despite small size, they’re energetic and love interactive games, fetch, and puzzle toys
  • Gentle: Excellent with children, seniors, and other pets when properly socialized
  • Alert: Make excellent watchdogs—will bark to alert you of visitors (but not excessive barkers)
  • Adaptable: Equally happy in apartments or houses with yards

Social Behavior: Maltipoos inherited the Poodle’s social intelligence and Maltese’s people-oriented nature. They:

  • Get along well with children (best with kids 6+)
  • Coexist peacefully with other dogs and cats
  • Enjoy meeting new people (not aggressive or overly shy)
  • Can experience separation anxiety if left alone for long periods (8+ hours)

Ideal for:

  • First-time dog owners
  • Families with children
  • Seniors seeking companionship
  • Apartment dwellers
  • People with mild to moderate dog allergies

Feeding & Nutrition Guidelines

Puppy Feeding Schedule (8 weeks – 6 months):

  • Transition to adult formula at 12 months

Senior Feeding (7+ years):

  • 2 meals daily
  • Switch to senior formula with joint support
  • Monitor weight and adjust portions as metabolism slows

Feeding Tips:

  • Always provide fresh water
  • Avoid table scraps (can cause digestive upset and obesity)
  • Use puzzle feeders or slow-feed bowls to prevent gulping
  • Treats should be <10% of daily calories
  • Monitor for food allergies (itching, ear infections, upset stomach)

Foods to AVOID (Toxic to Dogs):

  • Chocolate, caffeine, alcohol
  • Grapes, raisins, onions, garlic
  • Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
  • Macadamia nuts, avocado
  • Cooked bones (splinter risk)

Section 14: Why You Should Never Buy from Pet Stores or Puppy Mills

The Dark Truth About Pet Store Puppies

What Are Puppy Mills?

Puppy mills are commercial breeding operations that prioritize profit over animal welfare. They produce puppies in large quantities with:

  • No health testing (parents not screened for genetic diseases)
  • Poor living conditions (overcrowded cages, minimal human interaction)
  • Frequent breeding (females bred every heat cycle, leading to exhaustion and health issues)
  • Early weaning (puppies removed from mothers at 4-6 weeks instead of 8+ weeks)
  • No socialization (puppies isolated, resulting in fear and behavioral problems)

Early Neurological Stimulation (ENS): Days 3-16

What Is ENS? ENS (also called “Super Dog” training) involves five gentle exercises performed daily from day 3 to day 16 of life. These exercises introduce mild stressors that stimulate the puppy’s neurological system during a critical development window.

The 5 ENS Exercises (3-5 seconds each):

  1. Tactile Stimulation: Gently tickle between toes with cotton swab
  2. Head Held Up: Hold puppy upright with head above tail
  3. Head Pointed Down: Hold puppy head-down, nose toward floor
  4. Supine Position: Hold puppy on back in palm of hand
  5. Thermal Stimulation: Place puppy on cool damp towel (not cold)

Benefits of ENS:

  • Improved cardiovascular performance
  • Stronger heartbeats
  • Greater tolerance to stress
  • Enhanced immune system function
  • Better problem-solving abilities

MFS Implementation: Lawrence performs ENS exercises on every puppy daily from days 3-16. We’ve seen remarkable results—our puppies adjust to new homes faster and exhibit less anxiety than puppies from breeders who skip ENS.
