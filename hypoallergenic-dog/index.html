H2: What Makes a Dog Truly Hypoallergenic? Understanding Allergens, Dander, and Low-Shedding Coats

H3: The Science Behind Dog Allergies—It’s Not What You Think

Many potential dog owners mistakenly believe that dog allergies are triggered by fur or hair itself. In reality, allergic reactions stem from three primary sources: dander (microscopic dead skin cells), saliva proteins, and urine. When dogs shed their coats, they release dander into the air, which becomes airborne and settles on furniture, clothing, and surfaces throughout your home.

Hypoallergenic dogs like Maltipoos and Maltese produce significantly less dander than traditional shedding breeds like Labrador Retrievers or German Shepherds. Here’s why: instead of having a double-layered fur coat that sheds seasonally, hypoallergenic breeds possess hair rather than fur—and yes, there’s a critical difference.

H4: Hair vs. Fur—The Hypoallergenic Difference

Fur Characteristics:

  • Grows to a predetermined length, then sheds
  • Double-layered (topcoat + undercoat)
  • Sheds heavily 2-3 times per year
  • Traps and releases large amounts of dander

Hair Characteristics:

  • Grows continuously like human hair
  • Single-layered coat
  • Minimal shedding (only during brushing)
  • Traps dander within the coat until grooming

Maltipoos and Maltese both have hair-like coats inherited from their Poodle and Maltese lineage, making them ideal allergy-friendly dogs. Their continuously-growing hair sheds at a dramatically slower rate—approximately every 3-4 weeks compared to daily shedding in fur-coated breeds.
