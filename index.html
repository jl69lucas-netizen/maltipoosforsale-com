Essential Maltipoo Training Tips – How to Raise a Well-Behaved Companion from Day One

Maltipoos inherit the Poodle’s intelligence and the Maltese’s occasional stubborn streak—making proper training techniques essential for a well-behaved adult dog. Starting training correctly during the first weeks home prevents behavior problems that become harder to fix later. These proven training strategies from our 15 years of experience help you succeed from day one.

The Golden Rules of Maltipoo Training

Training your Maltipoo is less about “discipline” and more about learning to speak their language of treats and tail wags. These MFS-approved “Golden Rules” tap into your puppy’s natural intelligence to build a bond of trust that lasts far beyond the first month home.

Rule #1: Start Immediately: Training begins the moment your puppy arrives home. Every interaction teaches something—make sure you’re teaching what you want.

Rule #2: Use Positive Reinforcement only: Maltipoos are sensitive dogs who shut down with harsh corrections. Reward desired behaviors with treats, praise, and play. Ignore or redirect unwanted behaviors—never punish.

Rule #3: Keep Sessions Short: Puppies have short attention spans. Five 5-minute sessions throughout the day work better than one 25-minute session.

Rule #4: Be Consistent: Use the same commands, same rules, same rewards every time. Inconsistency confuses puppies and slows learning.

Rule #5: Socialize Early and often: Expose your puppy to different people, sounds, surfaces, and experiences between 8-16 weeks. This critical socialization window shapes adult temperament.

Priority Training for the First Month

Week 1: Foundation

Name recognition (say name + treat when puppy looks)

Crate training basics (positive association with crate)

Potty spot introduction (consistent location)

Handling (touch ears, paws, mouth daily)

Week 2: Basic Commands

“Sit” – Use a treat lure, reward immediately

Leash introduction – Let puppy drag leash indoors

Expanded crate time – Gradual duration increases

Continued socialization

Week 3: Building Duration

“Down” – From sit position, lure to lying position

“Stay” – Start with 2-3 seconds, gradually increase

Leash walking basics – Reward for staying near you

Visitor introductions

Week 4: Real-World Application

Commands in different locations

Outdoor leash walking

Public socialization (pet-friendly stores)

Alone time practice (preventing separation anxiety)

Our comprehensive training guide provides detailed instructions for each skill.

Common Maltipoo Training Challenges (and Solutions)

Challenge: Barking at visitors

Solution: Train the “quiet” command. Reward silence. Don’t yell (adds to excitement). Desensitize with controlled visitor introductions.

Challenge: Refusing to walk on a leash

Solution: Start indoors. Make leash positive (treats while wearing it). Let the puppy lead initially. Never drag.

Challenge: Jumping on people

Solution: Turn away and ignore jumping. Reward four-on-floor behavior. Ask visitors to do the same.

Challenge: Separation anxiety

Solution: Practice brief departures. Don’t make arrivals/departures dramatic. Provide enrichment toys when alone. Build duration gradually.

Challenge: Picky eating

Solution: Set food down for 15 minutes, then remove. No treats between meals. Healthy dogs don’t starve themselves.

Aggression toward people or other animals

Fear-based behaviors are not improving with socialization

House training regression after initial success

Any behavior causing safety concerns
