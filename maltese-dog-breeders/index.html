Deep Dive: The Maltese Training Journey (Delicacy and Discipline)

Successfully integrating your tiny Maltese puppy into your home requires a specialized approach that respects their small size and spirited temperament.

1. Training Challenges: House Training and Crate Training

House Training: The number one challenge for all toy breeds. Due to their small bladder capacity and the distraction of their loving nature, consistency is paramount. We recommend:

  • Pee Pad/Litter Box Transition: Use the litter box training foundation we start to smoothly transition to outdoor-only elimination.
  • Strict Schedule: Stick to a clockwork schedule—every 20 minutes when awake.
  • Safety First: Due to their small size, always potty train outdoors on a leash for safety, especially in busy areas like Miami, FL.

Crate Training: The crate is a safe den and a tool to accelerate house training. For the Maltese, the crate also prevents injury from falls or rough play when unsupervised.

  • Maltese Crate Size: Must be very small, just big enough to stand, turn, and lie down. Anything larger encourages them to eliminate in a corner.
  • Never Use the Crate as Punishment: Use positive association only.

2. Preventing “Small Dog Syndrome” in the Regal Maltese

Because the Maltese is so small and cute, owners often skip crucial discipline steps, leading to problem behaviors (excessive barking, aggression towards larger dogs, resource guarding).

  • No Free Reign: Do not allow your Maltese to constantly jump or bark for attention. Reward quiet, calm behavior.
  • Leash Manners: Even a small dog should walk politely on a leash. Do not carry your Maltese everywhere, as this deprives them of essential social and environmental exposure.
  • Consistency: The “bossy” streak is managed by consistent, kind boundaries. They are highly intelligent and will quickly test limits.

The MFS Grooming Philosophy: Preserving the Snow-White Coat

The Maltese coat is their crowning glory but requires one of the highest levels of commitment of any breed.

Step-by-Step Grooming Maintenance

  1. Daily Line Brushing: You must brush the coat in sections, all the way down to the skin, using the metal comb to ensure no mats are forming at the root. If left for 48 hours, a mat can form and quickly become painful.
  2. Tear Stain Management: The red-brown tear staining is caused by yeast and bacteria around the eyes. We recommend using a high-quality filter (or distilled) water, an appropriate diet, and daily enzymatic wipes to keep the area dry and clean.
  3. The Puppy Cut: Many owners, especially those in warmer climates like Arizona, opt for a shorter “puppy cut” to manage the high maintenance, but this still requires professional grooming every 4-6 weeks and brushing a few times a week.
