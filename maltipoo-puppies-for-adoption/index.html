Adopt Maltipoo with crate training foundation already established—our puppies understand crates as safe spaces and are 70-90% housetrained upon adoption. Complete housetraining requires consistency:

  • Take puppies outside immediately after waking, eating, drinking, and playing
  • Use consistent “potty” commands and reward successes immediately with treats

  • Never punish accidents—interrupt and redirect to appropriate areas

2. SOCIALIZATION (WEEKS 9-20 CRITICAL WINDOW)

Adopt Maltipoo Puppy Culture certified means puppies receive early neurological stimulation and socialization before adoption, but the 12-20 week window requires continued exposure to:

  • Various people (different ages, genders, ethnicities)
  • Other vaccinated, friendly dogs at puppy classes
  • Novel environments (car rides, different flooring surfaces, stairs)
  • Common sounds (vacuum cleaners, doorbells, traffic noise)

Proper socialization prevents fear-based behaviors and creates confident adult dogs who handle life’s challenges calmly.

3. BASIC OBEDIENCE (START IMMEDIATELY)

Intelligent Poodle genetics make Maltipoos eager learners. Start with:

  • Sit: Easiest command, teaches impulse control
  • Come: Critical for safety, practice with high-value treats
  • Stay: Develops patience and focus
  • Down: Calming command useful for settling excited puppies

Keep training sessions short (5-10 minutes) and positive. Your adopted Maltipoo arrives crate-trained, but maintain this by:

  • Using crates for safe containment when unsupervised
  • Never using crates as punishment
  • Feeding meals in crates to build positive associations
  • Covering crates with blankets for den-like security

5. PREVENTING SEPARATION ANXIETY

Maltipoos bond intensely with families, making separation anxiety common. Prevent this by:

  • Practicing short departures (5-10 minutes) from day one
  • Not making arrivals/departures emotional events
  • Providing puzzle toys and enrichment during alone time
  • Gradually extending alone time as puppies mature
