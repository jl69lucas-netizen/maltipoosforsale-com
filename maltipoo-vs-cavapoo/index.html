Maltipoo vs Cavapoo: Which Designer Breed Should You Buy for Your Family?

📌 Quick Answer:

Cavapoos are calmer, affectionate lap dogs ideal for quiet homes and seniors. Maltipoos are slightly more adaptable, easier to find from ethical breeders, and better for first-time owners wanting a hypoallergenic companion. Both are excellent apartment dogs, but the Maltipoo’s Poodle genetics often make them easier to train and less prone to Cavalier King Charles health issues.

🧮 Quick Comparison: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo at a Glance

Choosing between a Maltipoo and a Cavapoo is one of the most common dilemmas for families searching for a small, hypoallergenic designer breed. Both are adorable, affectionate, and apartment-friendly—but they have distinct personality differences, health considerations, and suitability factors that matter when you’re ready to invest in a lifelong companion.

If you’re ready to buy a Maltipoo puppy, this detailed comparison helps you understand whether the Maltipoo’s Maltese-Poodle genetics or the Cavapoo’s Cavalier King Charles-Poodle lineage better fits your lifestyle, home environment, and family needs. At MFS, we’ve helped hundreds of families compare these two breeds based on 20+ years of breeding experience.

Throughout this guide, you’ll find:

  • Side-by-side temperament and behavior comparisons
  • Health risk analysis (Mitral Valve Disease, Patellar Luxation, PRA)
  • Grooming and coat maintenance differences
  • Training difficulty and intelligence levels
  • Price breakdowns and ethical breeder guidance
  • A decision framework to help you choose confidently

Understanding the Maltipoo and Cavapoo: Origins, Parent Breeds & Designer Dog Appeal

Both the Maltipoo and Cavapoo are designer breeds—intentional crossbreeds created to combine the best traits of their purebred parents while reducing genetic health risks through hybrid vigor. Unlike purebred Poodles, these mixes offer softer temperaments and varied coat types that appeal to families seeking low-shedding companions.

The Maltipoo: Maltese + Poodle Heritage

The Maltipoo combines the elegant, affectionate Maltese with the intelligent, hypoallergenic Toy or Miniature Poodle. This cross emerged in the 1990s as breeders sought to create a small companion dog that retained the Maltese’s gentle, people-oriented nature while gaining the Poodle’s low-shedding coat and trainability. The Maltese parent contributes emotional sensitivity and adaptability, while the Poodle parent adds intelligence and structural robustness.

Maltipoos are particularly popular for apartment living, first-time dog owners, and families with mild to moderate allergies. Their size typically ranges from 5-12 pounds when bred with Toy Poodles, making them highly portable. Toy Maltipoos for sale are especially sought after for their manageable size and adaptable temperament.

The Cavapoo: Cavalier King Charles Spaniel + Poodle Heritage

The Cavapoo blends the affectionate, calm Cavalier King Charles Spaniel with the Poodle’s intelligence and hypoallergenic coat. Originating in Australia in the 1990s, Cavapoos were designed to be therapy dogs and emotional support animals due to the Cavalier’s naturally gentle, intuitive temperament. The Cavalier parent contributes a “lap dog” personality—extremely calm, patient, and ideal for seniors or quiet households.

Cavapoos tend to be slightly larger than Maltipoos (9-25 pounds depending on whether a Toy or Miniature Poodle is used) and have a softer, wavier coat texture. They’re excellent for families seeking a low-energy companion who thrives on affection and doesn’t require extensive exercise. However, the Cavalier lineage introduces specific health concerns—particularly Mitral Valve Disease (MVD), a hereditary heart condition that affects up to 50% of Cavaliers by age 5.

Why Families Compare Maltipoo vs Cavapoo

Both breeds appeal to similar buyer personas: apartment dwellers, allergy sufferers, seniors, and first-time dog owners. The decision usually comes down to:

  • Energy level preference: Do you want a playful, moderately active dog (Maltipoo) or a calmer lap dog (Cavapoo)?
  • Health risk tolerance: Are you comfortable managing potential Cavalier-related heart issues?
  • Coat hypoallergenic quality: Do you need maximum allergy-friendliness (Maltipoo F1B) or can you manage minimal shedding (Cavapoo F1)?
  • Price and availability: Maltipoos are often more affordable and easier to find from ethical breeders compared to Cavapoos.

Understanding which breed fits your lifestyle requires evaluating temperament, grooming needs, training ease, and long-term health commitments—all of which we’ll break down in the sections below.

Maltipoo vs Cavapoo Size, Weight & Physical Appearance: Which Breed Stays Smaller?

🧮 Adult Size Predictor: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo

💡 Pro Tip: Teacup and Toy Maltipoo sizes remain more consistent due to the Maltese parent’s small, stable size. Cavapoo size varies more widely depending on Poodle parent size.

Size Consistency: Maltipoos Are More Predictable

One of the biggest practical differences between these breeds is size predictability. Maltipoos bred from Toy Poodles typically mature to 5-12 pounds, with most falling in the 7-10 pound range. Mini Maltipoos bred from Miniature Poodles stay slightly larger at 10-15 pounds. Because the Maltese parent is consistently small (4-7 pounds), there’s less variation in the final adult size.

Cavapoos show wider size ranges because the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel parent is larger (13-18 pounds) and introduces more genetic variability. A Cavapoo from a Toy Poodle might weigh 9-15 pounds, while one from a Miniature Poodle could reach 20-25 pounds—a significant difference if you’re planning for apartment living or have size restrictions.

For buyers seeking the smallest possible companion, the Maltipoo offers more reliable sizing, especially in Teacup and Toy categories.

Physical Appearance: Coat Texture, Color & Structure

Maltipoo Appearance: Maltipoos typically have a soft, wavy to curly coat with a silkier texture than pure Poodles. Common colors include white, cream, apricot, silver, and parti (two-tone). Their face retains the Maltese’s sweet, expressive eyes and slightly rounded head, while the body structure is more robust than the delicate Maltese frame. The coat doesn’t shed but requires regular brushing to prevent matting.

Cavapoo Appearance: Cavapoos usually have a softer, wavier coat (less curly than Maltipoos) with a plush, teddy-bear appearance. Colors include Blenheim (chestnut and white), tricolor, ruby, black, and apricot—often with the Cavalier’s signature facial markings. Their ears are longer and more pendant-like (inherited from the Cavalier), and their overall build is slightly stockier than the Maltipoo’s.

Which Breed Looks More “Puppy-Like” Into Adulthood?

Both breeds retain puppy-like features well into adulthood, but Maltipoos tend to have a more refined, elegant appearance due to the Maltese influence, while Cavapoos maintain a rounder, “teddy bear” face due to the Cavalier’s broader skull structure. If you prefer the classic white or cream “fluffy cloud” look, Maltipoos are more likely to produce these colors consistently.

Buyer Consideration: If you’re buying a puppy for a child and want maximum “cute factor,” Cavapoos often win in the teddy-bear category. If you prefer a more elegant, refined companion, Maltipoos offer that sophisticated small-dog aesthetic.

Maltipoo vs Cavapoo Temperament: Personality, Family Fit & Behavioral Differences

This is where the Maltipoo vs Cavapoo decision becomes most personal. Temperament determines daily life quality, training ease, and long-term compatibility with your household. Both breeds are known for affectionate, people-oriented personalities, but they express affection differently and have distinct energy patterns.

Maltipoo Temperament: Playful, Adaptable & Emotionally Intuitive

Maltipoos inherit the Maltese’s deeply affectionate, people-focused nature combined with the Poodle’s intelligence and playfulness. They are:

  • Moderately energetic: Enjoy 30-45 minutes of daily activity (walks, play, mental stimulation) but are equally content cuddling on the couch.
  • Highly adaptable: Adjust well to apartment living, suburban homes, and even traveling. They’re portable and comfortable in varied environments.
  • Alert but not yappy: Will bark to alert you of visitors but aren’t typically chronic barkers when properly socialized.
  • Emotionally sensitive: Form strong bonds with their primary caregiver and can experience mild separation anxiety if left alone for extended periods (6+ hours).
  • Good with children: Patient with kids but do best with children over 6 who understand gentle handling. Early socialization is key.
  • Multi-pet friendly: Generally get along well with other dogs and can coexist with cats when introduced properly.

Best Maltipoo Owner Profile: First-time dog owners, apartment dwellers, working professionals who can provide midday breaks, families with school-age children, and anyone seeking a “velcro dog” who wants to be involved in daily activities.

Cavapoo Temperament: Calm, Gentle & Ultimate Lap Dog

Cavapoos inherit the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel’s exceptionally calm, gentle temperament—making them one of the most relaxed designer breeds. They are:

  • Low to moderate energy: Require only 20-40 minutes of daily activity and are perfectly content with short walks and indoor play.
  • Extremely affectionate: Thrive on physical closeness and are happiest when sitting on or beside their owner. True “lap dogs” in every sense.
  • Very quiet: Bark minimally, making them excellent for apartment living where noise is a concern.
  • Patient and gentle: Excellent with young children, elderly family members, and even infants due to their tolerant, non-reactive nature.
  • Lower separation anxiety: While still attached to their owners, Cavapoos handle alone time slightly better than Maltipoos due to the Cavalier’s more independent streak.
  • Therapy dog qualities: Their intuitive, calming presence makes them ideal emotional support animals and therapy dogs.

Best Cavapoo Owner Profile: Seniors, retirees, work-from-home professionals, families with young children or special needs members, and anyone seeking a calm, low-maintenance companion who doesn’t require extensive exercise.

Energy Level Comparison: Who Needs More Exercise?

If you’re an active person who enjoys daily walks, hikes, or outdoor adventures, the Maltipoo better matches that lifestyle. While less energetic than Cockapoos, Maltipoos appreciate varied activities and mental stimulation through training or puzzle toys.

If you prefer a quieter lifestyle, work long hours, or have mobility limitations, the Cavapoo’s lower energy needs and extreme cuddle-orientation make them the better fit. They’re content with short bathroom breaks and indoor play.

Which Breed Is Better for Apartments?

Both excel in apartments, but for different reasons:

  • Maltipoo advantage: Smaller size options (5-10 lbs), quieter than most small breeds, and highly adaptable to limited space.
  • Cavapoo advantage: Even lower energy, minimal barking, and completely content with indoor-only days when weather is poor.

If you’re buying a puppy for city apartment living, either breed works—but consider whether you want a companion who encourages you to get outside (Maltipoo) or one who’s happiest staying indoors with you (Cavapoo).

Separation Anxiety: Which Breed Handles Alone Time Better?

Both breeds form strong attachments and prefer not to be alone for long periods. However:

  • Maltipoos can develop more intense separation anxiety due to the Maltese’s “velcro dog” genetics. They need gradual crate training and should not be left alone for more than 4-6 hours regularly.
  • Cavapoos handle alone time slightly better but still shouldn’t be left isolated for full workdays without a midday break or pet sitter.

Solution for working owners: Both breeds benefit from early crate training, interactive toys, and doggy daycare if you work full-time outside the home.

Grooming, Shedding & Hypoallergenic Qualities: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo Coat Care

For allergy sufferers and low-maintenance seekers, coat care is a critical deciding factor. While both breeds are marketed as “hypoallergenic” and “non-shedding,” there are meaningful differences in coat texture, maintenance requirements, and true hypoallergenic reliability.

Maltipoo Coat: Curly, Low-Shedding & Maximum Hypoallergenic

Maltipoos are among the most reliably hypoallergenic small breeds because the Poodle’s curly coat gene is dominant. Most Maltipoos (especially F1B generation—75% Poodle) have tightly curled or wavy coats that trap loose hair rather than releasing it into the environment. This makes them ideal for moderate to severe allergy sufferers.

Maltipoo Grooming Requirements:

  • Brushing: 2-3 times per week to prevent matting (curly coats tangle easily)
  • Professional grooming: Every 6-8 weeks for trimming and shaping
  • Bathing: Every 3-4 weeks or as needed
  • Ear cleaning: Weekly (to prevent infections in floppy-eared breeds)
  • Nail trimming: Every 3-4 weeks
  • Dental care: Daily brushing recommended (small breeds are prone to dental issues)

Annual grooming cost estimate: $600-$900 (8-10 professional grooming sessions at $60-90 each, plus at-home supplies)

If you prefer DIY grooming, Maltipoos are manageable with clippers and basic grooming tools, though the curly coat requires practice to trim evenly.

Cavapoo Coat: Wavy, Soft & Less Hypoallergenic

Cavapoos typically have a softer, wavier coat (less curly than Maltipoos) because the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel parent has a straight, silky coat. F1 Cavapoos (50% Cavalier, 50% Poodle) are less predictably hypoallergenic—some shed lightly, especially during seasonal changes. For maximum hypoallergenic qualities, you’d need an F1B Cavapoo (75% Poodle), which are harder to find.

Cavapoo Grooming Requirements:

  • Brushing: 3-4 times per week (wavy coats mat faster than curly ones)
  • Professional grooming: Every 6-8 weeks
  • Bathing: Every 3-4 weeks
  • Ear cleaning: Weekly (Cavaliers have longer, more infection-prone ears)
  • Eye cleaning: Daily (Cavaliers are prone to tear staining)
  • Nail trimming: Every 3-4 weeks
  • Dental care: Daily brushing

Annual grooming cost estimate: $700-$1,000 (slightly higher due to more frequent brushing needs and tear stain maintenance)

Hypoallergenic Comparison: Which Breed Is Better for Allergies?

If you or a family member has moderate to severe dog allergies, the Maltipoo is the safer choice—especially F1B Maltipoos, which are 75% Poodle and produce minimal dander. MFS specializes in F1B Maltipoos for allergy sufferers, ensuring both parents are health-tested and low-shedding.

Cavapoos are suitable for mild allergies but are not as reliably hypoallergenic as Maltipoos due to the Cavalier’s shedding coat genetics. If allergies are a primary concern, verify the Cavapoo’s generation (F1B is best) and ask the breeder about parent coat types.

Grooming Difficulty: Which Is Easier to Maintain?

Both breeds require similar professional grooming schedules, but:

  • Maltipoos need less frequent brushing (2-3x/week vs 3-4x/week) and have fewer tear staining issues.
  • Cavapoos need more diligent eye cleaning and are more prone to matting if brushing is missed.

For first-time dog owners, the Maltipoo’s slightly easier grooming routine can be a deciding factor.

Coat Color Maintenance

Both breeds come in multiple colors, but white and cream coats (common in both) show tear staining and require daily facial cleaning. White Maltipoos and Cavapoos benefit from filtered water, high-quality grain-free diets, and regular grooming to minimize staining.

Health, Lifespan & Genetic Risks: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo Long-Term Wellness

Understanding breed-specific health risks is essential when choosing a companion you’ll care for 12-15+ years. While hybrid vigor (genetic diversity from crossing two breeds) reduces some purebred health issues, both Maltipoos and Cavapoos inherit vulnerabilities from their parent breeds. Ethical breeders like MFS provide health guarantees and conduct comprehensive genetic testing to minimize these risks.

Maltipoo Health Profile: Generally Robust with Minor Concerns

Common Health Issues in Maltipoos:

  • Patellar Luxation: Kneecap dislocation common in small breeds (inherited from both Maltese and Poodle lines). OFA screening of parent dogs reduces risk.
  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA): Genetic eye condition that can lead to blindness. DNA testing identifies carriers before breeding.
  • White Shaker Syndrome: Rare neurological condition causing tremors (more common in white Maltese). Usually manageable with medication.
  • Dental Disease: Small mouths lead to crowding and tartar buildup. Daily tooth brushing is essential.
  • Hypoglycemia: Low blood sugar risk in very small puppies (Teacup/Toy). Managed with frequent feeding schedules.

Maltipoo Lifespan: 12-15 years (some live to 16-18 with excellent care)

Maltipoo Health Advantages: Because both parent breeds (Maltese and Poodle) are generally healthy, Maltipoos benefit from strong hybrid vigor. Compared to purebred Maltese, Maltipoos have sturdier bone structures and fewer fragility issues.

Cavapoo Health Profile: More Serious Hereditary Concerns

Common Health Issues in Cavapoos:

  • Mitral Valve Disease (MVD): THE most significant concern. This progressive heart condition affects 50%+ of Cavalier King Charles Spaniels by age 5 and 100% by age 10. While crossing with Poodles dilutes the risk, Cavapoos still have higher MVD rates than most small breeds. Early detection through annual cardiac exams is critical.
  • Syringomyelia (SM): Neurological condition where fluid-filled cavities form in the spinal cord near the brain. Causes severe pain and is unfortunately common in Cavaliers. Responsible breeders MRI-scan parent dogs to screen for this.
  • Hip Dysplasia: More common in Cavapoos than Maltipoos due to the Cavalier’s structural build. OFA hip scoring identifies at-risk breeding dogs.
  • Eye Issues: Cataracts, cherry eye, and dry eye occur more frequently in Cavapoos. Annual ophthalmology exams recommended.
  • Patellar Luxation: Similar risk as Maltipoos.
  • Ear Infections: Cavaliers’ long, heavy ears trap moisture, leading to chronic infections if not cleaned weekly.

Cavapoo Lifespan: 12-15 years (though MVD can shorten lifespan if not managed)

Cavapoo Health Disadvantage: The Cavalier King Charles Spaniel is one of the least healthy purebred dog breeds due to decades of inbreeding and prioritizing appearance over health. Even with Poodle hybrid vigor, Cavapoos carry a higher disease burden than Maltipoos.

Which Breed Has Lower Veterinary Costs Over a Lifetime?

Maltipoos typically have lower lifetime vet costs because their primary health concerns (patellar luxation, PRA) are either preventable through screening or manageable with routine care. Annual vet costs for a healthy Maltipoo: $300-$600.

Cavapoos require more expensive monitoring and potential treatments:

  • Annual cardiac ultrasounds for MVD monitoring: $300-$500/year
  • MRI for syringomyelia diagnosis: $2,000-$3,500 (if symptoms appear)
  • Heart medications if MVD progresses: $50-$150/month

Annual vet costs for a healthy Cavapoo: $500-$900 (higher due to cardiac monitoring). If health issues develop, costs can exceed $2,000-$5,000 annually.

Importance of Health-Tested Parents

When buying from MFS, all parent dogs undergo:

  • OFA Patellar Luxation screening
  • DNA testing for PRA and other genetic conditions
  • Cardiac auscultation (heart listening exam)
  • Hip and elbow evaluations (for breeding dogs over 2 years)
  • Annual comprehensive health exams

For Cavapoo buyers, ALWAYS ask for proof of parent cardiac clearances and MRI results for syringomyelia. Reputable Cavapoo breeders invest $1,000+ per parent dog in health screening—if a breeder can’t provide these results, walk away.

Training, Intelligence & Obedience: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo Ease of Training

Both breeds are highly trainable and eager to please, but they respond to training methods differently due to their parent breeds’ distinct intelligence types and motivations. Understanding how each breed learns helps you set realistic expectations and choose appropriate training approaches.

Maltipoo Intelligence & Training Style

Maltipoos inherit the Poodle’s high intelligence (ranked #2 in Stanley Coren’s dog intelligence rankings) combined with the Maltese’s eagerness to please. This creates a dog that:

  • Learns quickly: Grasps basic commands (sit, stay, come) within 5-10 repetitions.
  • Responds well to positive reinforcement: Highly food-motivated and praise-motivated.
  • Can be independent thinkers: Poodle genetics mean they sometimes problem-solve rather than simply obeying (e.g., finding creative ways to get treats).
  • Needs mental stimulation: Boredom leads to nuisance behaviors like excessive barking or chewing. Puzzle toys and training games are essential.
  • Housetraining timeline: Typically fully housetrained by 4-6 months with consistent crate training and scheduled bathroom breaks.

Training Difficulty Rating: 2/5 (Easy) — Suitable for first-time dog owners who commit to daily 10-15 minute training sessions.

Best Training Methods for Maltipoos: Clicker training, treat-based positive reinforcement, short (5-10 minute) sessions to maintain focus, and early socialization to prevent timidity around strangers.

Cavapoo Intelligence & Training Style

Cavapoos combine the Poodle’s intelligence with the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel’s gentle, compliant nature—making them arguably the easiest small breed to train. They:

  • Are extremely eager to please: Cavalier genetics create a dog that wants nothing more than to make you happy.
  • Rarely stubborn: Unlike some intelligent breeds that can be willful, Cavapoos are naturally obedient.
  • Respond to gentle corrections: Harsh training methods are unnecessary and counterproductive—soft verbal corrections are sufficient.
  • Excel at therapy/service work: Their intuitive nature makes them ideal for emotional support and basic service tasks.
  • Housetraining timeline: Often fully housetrained by 3-5 months due to their compliance and routine-loving nature.

Training Difficulty Rating: 1.5/5 (Very Easy) — Perfect for first-time dog owners, seniors, or families with children who want to participate in training.

Best Training Methods for Cavapoos: Gentle positive reinforcement, consistency in routine, and calm, patient instruction. Cavapoos thrive on routine and become anxious with inconsistent rules or schedules.

Which Breed Is Better for First-Time Dog Owners?

Both are excellent for beginners, but:

  • Maltipoo: Better if you want a trainable dog that still has personality and energy. They challenge you just enough to make training rewarding without being difficult.
  • Cavapoo: Better if you want the absolute easiest training experience and have a calm household. Their extreme compliance means fewer training frustrations.

Behavioral Issues: Barking, Separation Anxiety & Destructiveness

Maltipoos: Can develop nuisance barking if not trained early. Their alert nature means they’ll announce visitors, delivery drivers, and passing dogs. Early socialization reduces fear-based barking. Separation anxiety is manageable with gradual crate training.

Cavapoos: Quieter by nature but can develop depression-like behaviors if left alone too long (sleeping excessively, loss of appetite). Their clinginess means they need human companionship most of the day.

Both breeds benefit from:

  • Crate training starting at 8 weeks
  • Gradual alone-time desensitization
  • Interactive toys (Kongs, puzzle feeders) when you’re away
  • Regular exercise before alone periods

Exercise Requirements & Energy Levels: Daily Activity Needs for Maltipoo vs Cavapoo

Understanding daily exercise needs helps you assess whether a breed fits your lifestyle. Active individuals might find a Cavapoo too sedentary, while busy professionals might struggle with a Maltipoo’s need for engagement.

Maltipoo Exercise Needs: Moderate Activity, Mental Stimulation Essential

Maltipoos need 30-45 minutes of daily activity split between:

  • Two 15-20 minute walks
  • 10-15 minutes of play (fetch, tug, interactive toys)
  • Mental stimulation (training, puzzle toys, sniff games)

They enjoy varied activities—neighborhood walks, visits to dog parks (once fully vaccinated), short hikes on easy trails, and indoor play during bad weather. Maltipoos are adaptable athletes—they’ll match your energy level but need daily engagement to prevent boredom behaviors.

Best For: Apartment dwellers who enjoy daily walks, work-from-home professionals who take midday breaks, retirees with moderate activity levels.

Cavapoo Exercise Needs: Low-Moderate Activity, Indoor Play Preferred

Cavapoos need 20-40 minutes of daily activity and are content with:

  • One or two short walks (10-15 minutes each)
  • Indoor play and cuddling
  • Gentle games (they’re not high-energy fetch enthusiasts)

They’re perfectly happy with minimal outdoor time, especially in extreme heat or cold. Many Cavapoo owners find their dogs prefer staying indoors on rainy days and skip walks without issue. Compared to Maltipoos, Cavapoos are less motivated by exercise and more motivated by proximity to their owner.

Best For: Seniors, people with limited mobility, families with very young children who need a calm companion, or anyone seeking a “couch potato” small breed.

Apartment Living: Which Breed Adapts Better?

Both thrive in apartments, but:

  • Maltipoo: Needs access to outdoor potty breaks 3-4x daily and benefits from apartment complexes with dog parks or green spaces nearby.
  • Cavapoo: Can handle bathroom breaks on a balcony potty pad if needed (though outdoor potty training is always preferred). Less demanding about outdoor exploration.

Can These Breeds Live Without a Yard?

Absolutely. Neither breed requires a fenced yard. Small dogs like Maltipoos and Cavapoos get sufficient exercise through leashed walks and indoor play. In fact, they’re TOO SMALL to be left unsupervised in yards due to predator risks (hawks, coyotes) and their inability to handle extreme temperatures.

  • Annual health testing: $500-$1,500 per dog
  • Quality food: $100-$150/month per dog
  • Veterinary care: $1,000-$2,000/year per dog
  • Puppy vaccinations/deworming: $150-$250 per puppy
  • Facility maintenance, supplies, labor

Breeders selling puppies for $800-$1,200 are cutting corners somewhere—usually health testing, veterinary care, or proper socialization.

Frequently Asked Questions: Maltipoo vs Cavapoo

Q: Which breed is better for families with young children?

A: Cavapoos edge out Maltipoos for families with children under 6 due to their exceptionally patient, gentle temperament and higher tolerance for rough handling. Maltipoos are great with children 6+ who understand gentle touch. Both breeds should always be supervised with toddlers due to small size and potential for accidental injury.

Q: Can Maltipoos and Cavapoos live with cats?

A: Yes, both breeds can coexist peacefully with cats when properly introduced. Neither has a strong prey drive. Early socialization with cats (ideally before 16 weeks) produces the best results. Supervise initial interactions and provide cats with escape routes.

Q: Which breed barks less?

A: Cavapoos are significantly quieter than Maltipoos. Cavapoos rarely bark except when absolutely necessary. Maltipoos are alert dogs and will bark to announce visitors, delivery drivers, or unusual sounds—though this is trainable and not excessive compared to terrier breeds.

Q: Are Maltipoos healthier than Cavapoos?

A: Generally, yes. Maltipoos benefit from two relatively healthy parent breeds (Maltese and Poodle), while Cavapoos inherit serious genetic risks from the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel—particularly Mitral Valve Disease (heart condition) and Syringomyelia (neurological condition). Maltipoos from health-tested parents typically have fewer long-term health issues and lower lifetime veterinary costs.

Q: Which breed is easier to potty train?

A: Cavapoos are slightly easier to potty train due to their eager-to-please nature and love of routine. They’re typically fully housetrained by 3-5 months. Maltipoos take 4-6 months on average but are still considered easy compared to most small breeds. Both respond well to crate training and scheduled bathroom breaks.

Q: Can I find Maltipoos or Cavapoos in rescues?

A: Designer breed rescues do exist, though availability is limited. Check breed-specific rescues like “Doodle Rescue Collective” or “Poodle Mix Rescue.” However, rescue dogs often lack health histories and may have behavioral issues requiring experienced owners. Buying from an ethical breeder like MFS ensures health testing, early socialization, and lifetime breeder support.

Q: Which breed travels better?

A: Maltipoos travel slightly better due to their smaller size (fits more easily in airline-approved carriers) and more adaptable temperament. Both breeds are small enough for in-cabin air travel. Cavapoos can become anxious in new environments more readily than Maltipoos, though early travel exposure helps both breeds become confident travelers.

Q: What’s the difference between F1, F1B, and F2 generations?

A:

  • F1: 50% Maltese/Cavalier, 50% Poodle (first-generation cross). Moderate shedding, less predictable coat.
  • F1B: 25% Maltese/Cavalier, 75% Poodle (F1 bred back to Poodle). Maximum hypoallergenic quality, curliest coat.
  • F2: F1 bred to F1. Least predictable—can shed more than F1.

For allergies, MFS recommends F1B Maltipoos for maximum non-shedding reliability.
