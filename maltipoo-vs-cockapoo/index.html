Maltipoo vs Cockapoo: Which Designer Breed Is Better for Active Families?

📌 Quick Answer:

Cockapoos are larger, more energetic dogs ideal for active families with children and yards. Maltipoos are smaller, calmer, and better suited for apartments, first-time owners, and allergy sufferers. Both are affectionate and trainable, but the Cockapoo’s Cocker Spaniel hunting instincts mean more exercise needs and potential prey drive, while the Maltipoo’s gentler temperament makes them easier for beginners.

🧮 Quick Comparison: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo at a Glance

Deciding between a Maltipoo and a Cockapoo often comes down to lifestyle energy levels and living space. Both are beloved designer breeds that combine Poodle intelligence with the affection of their companion-breed parents—but the Cockapoo’s Cocker Spaniel lineage introduces a sportier, more active personality that requires significantly more daily exercise and engagement than the gentler Maltipoo.

If you’re ready to buy a Maltipoo puppy, understanding how they differ from Cockapoos helps you make a confident choice. At MFS, we’ve helped hundreds of families compare these breeds based on 20+ years of breeding experience, and we consistently see the same questions: Which breed is calmer? Which sheds less? Which is better for kids? Which fits apartment living?

Throughout this comparison guide, you’ll discover:

  • Size differences and predictability (Cockapoos vary more widely)
  • Temperament analysis: hunting instincts vs companion genetics
  • Grooming intensity and hypoallergenic reliability
  • Health risks specific to Cocker Spaniel lines (PRA, ear infections)
  • Exercise needs and energy management strategies
  • Training difficulty and behavioral considerations
  • Price comparison and ethical breeder guidance

Understanding the Maltipoo vs Cockapoo: Parent Breed Heritage & Designer Dog Purpose

Both breeds emerged in the 1990s as intentional designer crosses aimed at combining Poodle hypoallergenic coats with beloved companion breeds. However, the Maltese and Cocker Spaniel parents bring vastly different genetic purposes—one bred for centuries as a lap dog, the other as a hunting retriever—which fundamentally shapes their offspring’s temperament and needs. Unlike Cavapoos which prioritize calmness, Cockapoos retain working-dog drive that active families appreciate but busy professionals may find challenging.

The Maltipoo: Maltese + Poodle = Ultimate Companion Dog

The Maltipoo crosses the ancient Maltese companion breed (bred for 2,000+ years as royalty’s lap dogs) with the intelligent Toy or Miniature Poodle. This combination creates a small, affectionate, people-oriented dog with minimal exercise needs and maximum adaptability. The Maltese parent contributes emotional sensitivity, gentle demeanor, and a preference for indoor comfort, while the Poodle adds trainability, structural robustness, and a non-shedding curly coat.

Maltipoos excel in apartments, with first-time owners, and in households seeking a “velcro dog” who wants to be involved in daily life without demanding intense physical activity. Toy Maltipoos typically mature to 5-10 pounds, making them highly portable and manageable for seniors or owners with mobility limitations.

The Cockapoo: Cocker Spaniel + Poodle = Energetic Family Dog

The Cockapoo blends the American or English Cocker Spaniel (bred as bird-hunting retrievers) with the Poodle’s intelligence and low-shedding coat. This creates a medium-small dog with athletic ability, higher exercise needs, and a more outgoing, social temperament than the Maltipoo. The Cocker Spaniel parent contributes a “merry” personality—exuberant, eager, and ready for outdoor adventures—along with a denser, wavy coat and potential prey drive toward small animals.

Cockapoos typically weigh 12-25 pounds depending on whether a Toy, Miniature, or Standard Poodle is used. They’re popular with families who have yards, enjoy hiking or jogging, and want a dog who can keep up with active children. However, the Cocker Spaniel lineage introduces specific health concerns—particularly Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA, an eye disease) and chronic ear infections—that require vigilant monitoring.

Why Families Compare Maltipoo vs Cockapoo

These breeds appeal to overlapping demographics (families wanting affectionate, trainable, low-shedding dogs), but the decision typically hinges on:

  • Activity level: Can you commit to 45-60 minutes of daily vigorous exercise (Cockapoo) or prefer 30-45 minutes of moderate activity (Maltipoo)?
  • Living space: Do you have a fenced yard (beneficial for Cockapoos) or live in an apartment (better for Maltipoos)?
  • Grooming time: Can you brush daily (Cockapoo’s denser coat) or prefer 2-3x weekly (Maltipoo)?
  • Allergy severity: Do you need maximum hypoallergenic reliability (Maltipoo F1B) or can you manage low shedding (Cockapoo F1)?
  • Training experience: Are you a first-time owner (Maltipoo is easier) or experienced with spirited breeds (Cockapoos can be stubborn)?

Understanding which breed matches your lifestyle prevents the common mistake of choosing based on appearance alone, then struggling with mismatched energy needs.

Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Size, Weight & Physical Differences: Which Breed Stays Smaller?

🧮 Adult Size Predictor: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo

💡 Pro Tip: Cockapoos show the widest size variation in designer breeds due to Cocker Spaniel variability (15-30 lbs). Maltipoo sizing is far more predictable because the Maltese parent is consistently small (4-7 lbs).

Size Predictability: Maltipoos Are More Consistent

One of the biggest practical differences is adult size consistency. Maltipoos bred from Toy Poodles reliably mature to 5-12 pounds, with most falling in the 7-10 pound range. Because the Maltese parent is standardized at 4-7 pounds, there’s minimal size variation—what you see at 8 weeks gives you a fairly accurate adult prediction.

Cockapoos show dramatic size ranges because Cocker Spaniels vary widely (American Cockers: 20-30 lbs; English Cockers: 26-34 lbs), and breeders use Toy, Miniature, or even Standard Poodles. A “Toy Cockapoo” might weigh 12 pounds or 18 pounds. A “Mini Cockapoo” could be 15 pounds or 25 pounds. This unpredictability matters if you have apartment weight restrictions (many limit dogs to 15-20 lbs) or physical limitations requiring a truly small dog.

Which Breed Is Truly “Small”?

If you want a pocket-sized companion, the Maltipoo is your breed. At 5-12 pounds, they’re genuinely lap-sized and easy to carry. Teacup Maltipoos (4-7 lbs) are among the smallest designer breeds available.

Cockapoos at 12-25 pounds are “small-to-medium”—too large for many airline-approved carriers (10-15 lb limit for in-cabin travel) and challenging to carry for extended periods. If portability, travel, or very limited space matter, Maltipoos offer significant advantages.

Physical Appearance: Coat Texture, Build & Structure

Maltipoo Appearance: Soft, wavy to curly coat with silky texture. Refined, elegant build with a slightly rounded head and expressive eyes. Colors include white, cream, apricot, silver, black, and parti (two-tone). The body is compact but surprisingly sturdy—not as delicate as the purebred Maltese. Ears are medium-length and folded. Overall impression: sophisticated small companion.

Cockapoo Appearance: Dense, wavy to loosely curled coat with plush texture (more “woolly” than Maltipoos). Stockier, more athletic build with a broader chest and longer legs. Colors include buff, red, black, chocolate, parti, merle, and roan. Ears are long and pendant (inherited from Cocker Spaniels), giving a “floppy-eared” appearance. Overall impression: sporty, teddy-bear-like family dog.

Coat Density Comparison

This is critical for grooming: Cockapoo coats are significantly denser than Maltipoo coats due to the Cocker Spaniel’s double-coat genetics. While Poodle dilutes this somewhat, Cockapoos still have more undercoat, which:

  • Mats faster if brushing is skipped
  • Traps more dirt and debris during outdoor play
  • Requires more frequent professional grooming (every 4-6 weeks vs 6-8 weeks)
  • Sheds more than Maltipoos (though still “low-shedding” compared to most breeds)

If you’re buying a puppy primarily for low-maintenance grooming, the Maltipoo’s single-layer, less dense coat is easier to manage.

Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Temperament: Companion Genetics vs Hunting Instincts

This is where the breeds diverge most significantly. Temperament determines daily compatibility, training challenges, and long-term happiness for both you and your dog. Understanding these innate behavioral differences helps you choose a breed that matches your energy and lifestyle naturally rather than requiring constant management.

Maltipoo Temperament: Gentle, Adaptable & Emotionally Attuned

Maltipoos are bred from two companion breeds (Maltese and Poodle), meaning their genetic purpose is singular: be an affectionate, human-focused companion. They are:

  • Moderately energetic: Enjoy daily walks and play but are equally content cuddling. Energy comes in bursts rather than sustained output.
  • Low prey drive: Rarely chase squirrels, cats, or birds. Safe around small pets when properly introduced.
  • Highly people-oriented: Prefer human company over independent activities. Form strong bonds with primary caregivers and can experience separation anxiety if left alone 6+ hours.
  • Alert but not aggressive: Will bark to announce visitors but aren’t territorial or protective. Typically friendly with strangers after initial wariness.
  • Gentle with children: Patient with respectful kids (ages 6+) but can be overwhelmed by rough toddler handling. Best in homes where children understand “gentle touch.”
  • Calm indoors: Once exercise needs are met, Maltipoos are quiet, content lap dogs who don’t demand constant stimulation.

Best Maltipoo Owner Profile: First-time dog owners, apartment dwellers, seniors, work-from-home professionals, introverts who want a low-key companion, families with school-age children.

Cockapoo Temperament: Outgoing, Energetic & Forever Playful

Cockapoos inherit the Cocker Spaniel’s hunting dog genetics—bred for stamina, enthusiasm, and retrieving drive. This creates a fundamentally different energy profile:

  • High sustained energy: Need 45-60 minutes of vigorous daily exercise (not just walks—running, fetch, swimming). Don’t “settle” as easily as Maltipoos.
  • Moderate prey drive: May chase squirrels, rabbits, or cats due to Cocker Spaniel instincts. Requires secure fencing and careful introductions to small pets.
  • Extremely social and outgoing: Love meeting new people and dogs. Rarely shy or reserved. Thrive in busy, active households.
  • Exuberant greeting behaviors: Prone to jumping, spinning, and excited vocalizations when owners come home. Requires training to manage.
  • Excellent with children: Robust enough for active play, patient with kids, and enjoy the chaos of family life. One of the best breeds for households with multiple young children.
  • Prone to “zoomies”: Sudden bursts of frenetic energy (running in circles, bouncing off furniture) are common, especially in puppies and young adults.

Best Cockapoo Owner Profile: Active families with children, outdoor enthusiasts (hikers, runners, campers), people with yards, extroverts who want a social companion, experienced dog owners who appreciate spirited personalities.

Energy Level Comparison: Who Needs More Exercise?

This is non-negotiable: Cockapoos require significantly more daily exercise than Maltipoos.

  • Maltipoo: 30-45 minutes daily (two 15-20 minute walks + indoor play). Can skip walks occasionally if weather is bad without behavioral issues.
  • Cockapoo: 45-60+ minutes daily (two walks PLUS active play like fetch, agility, or dog park time). Skipping exercise leads to destructive behaviors (chewing, digging, excessive barking).

If you work full-time, have mobility limitations, or simply prefer a lower-maintenance exercise routine, the Maltipoo is the practical choice. Cockapoos truly need active, outdoor-oriented owners.

Apartment Living: Which Breed Adapts Better?

Both CAN live in apartments, but:

  • Maltipoo: Thrives in apartments. Small size, moderate energy, quiet demeanor, and minimal space needs make them ideal urban dogs.
  • Cockapoo: Challenging in apartments without nearby dog parks or green spaces. Their energy needs and larger size mean they feel confined more easily. Success requires 3-4 daily outdoor trips for exercise and bathroom breaks.

If you’re buying a puppy for apartment living, the Maltipoo offers significantly easier management.

Behavioral Challenges: What Problems Arise?

Maltipoo challenges:

  • Separation anxiety if left alone too long
  • Alert barking (trainable but requires consistency)
  • Timidity with strangers if not socialized early
  • Dental issues requiring daily tooth brushing

Cockapoo challenges:

  • Destructive behaviors when under-exercised (chewing furniture, digging)
  • Jumping on people (requires firm training)
  • Prey drive toward cats, squirrels, birds
  • Chronic ear infections (inherited from Cocker Spaniels)
  • Stubbornness during training (Cocker Spaniel can be willful)
  • Excessive barking or whining when bored

For first-time owners, Maltipoo behavioral challenges are easier to prevent and manage than Cockapoo issues, which often stem from unmet exercise needs.

Grooming Intensity & Hypoallergenic Reliability: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Coat Care

Grooming is where many Cockapoo owners feel overwhelmed—the breed’s denser coat requires significantly more maintenance than most first-time owners anticipate. For allergy sufferers and low-maintenance seekers, these differences are critical decision factors.

Maltipoo Coat: Silky, Loosely Curled & Reliably Hypoallergenic

Maltipoos typically inherit a soft, wavy-to-curly coat that’s less dense than Cockapoos. The Maltese parent’s silky texture softens the Poodle’s tight curls, creating a coat that’s easier to brush and less prone to severe matting. F1B Maltipoos (75% Poodle) have curlier coats with maximum hypoallergenic reliability—ideal for moderate to severe allergies.

Maltipoo Grooming Requirements:

  • Brushing: 2-3 times per week (10-15 minutes per session). Daily brushing during shedding seasons (spring/fall) prevents tangles.
  • Professional grooming: Every 6-8 weeks for trimming, sanitary clips, and coat shaping ($60-90 per visit).
  • Bathing: Every 3-4 weeks or as needed with hypoallergenic dog shampoo.
  • Ear cleaning: Weekly using veterinary-approved ear solution.
  • Nail trimming: Every 3-4 weeks (or when you hear clicking on floors).
  • Dental care: Daily tooth brushing to prevent periodontal disease (small breeds are highly susceptible).

Annual grooming cost: $600-$900 (professional grooming) + $100-150 (at-home supplies)

Cockapoo Coat: Dense, Wavy & High-Maintenance

Cockapoos have significantly denser coats due to Cocker Spaniel double-coat genetics. Even with Poodle dilution, most Cockapoos retain thicker undercoats that mat quickly if brushing is missed even one or two days. The coat texture ranges from wavy to loosely curled, and it traps dirt, burrs, and debris during outdoor activities.

Cockapoo Grooming Requirements:

  • Brushing: DAILY (15-20 minutes) to prevent mats, especially around ears, armpits, and hindquarters. Missing even 2-3 days creates painful tangles.
  • Professional grooming: Every 4-6 weeks (more frequent than Maltipoos due to faster coat growth and density). Cost: $70-110 per visit.
  • Bathing: Every 2-3 weeks due to coat density and outdoor activity level.
  • Ear cleaning: 2-3 times per week. Cocker Spaniels’ heavy, floppy ears trap moisture, making ear infections extremely common. This is the #1 health issue in Cockapoos.
  • Eye cleaning: Daily—Cockapoos are prone to tear staining and eye discharge.
  • Nail trimming: Every 2-3 weeks (active outdoor dogs wear nails down faster).
  • Dental care: Daily brushing.

Annual grooming cost: $900-$1,400 (professional grooming) + $150-200 (at-home supplies)

Grooming Time Investment: The Reality

Let’s be honest about time commitments:

  • Maltipoo: 30-45 minutes weekly brushing + 1 hour every 6-8 weeks for grooming appointments = ~4-5 hours monthly
  • Cockapoo: 2+ hours weekly brushing + 1 hour every 4-6 weeks for grooming = ~10-12 hours monthly

For busy professionals, parents, or anyone with limited time, the Maltipoo’s grooming schedule is far more manageable.

Hypoallergenic Comparison: Which Sheds Less?

Neither breed is 100% hypoallergenic (no dog is—allergies react to dander, not just hair), but:

  • Maltipoo: Minimal shedding, especially F1B generations. Suitable for moderate to severe allergies. Dander production is low due to single-layer coat.
  • Cockapoo: Low-to-moderate shedding. F1 Cockapoos (50% Cocker, 50% Poodle) shed more than F1B (75% Poodle). Suitable for mild allergies only. The denser undercoat produces more dander.

If allergies are a primary concern, the Maltipoo is the safer, more reliable choice. Many families buy Cockapoos assuming they’re hypoallergenic, then struggle with allergy symptoms.

Ear Infection Risk: A Critical Cockapoo Concern

Cockapoos inherit the Cocker Spaniel’s heavy, floppy ears that cover the ear canal, creating a warm, moist environment perfect for bacterial and yeast infections. Chronic ear infections are THE most common health complaint from Cockapoo owners, requiring:

  • 2-3x weekly ear cleaning (not just checking—actual cleaning)
  • Annual vet bills for infection treatment: $200-500
  • Potential for surgery in severe cases: $1,000-3,000

Maltipoos have medium-length ears that still require weekly cleaning but have significantly lower infection rates.

Health Risks, Lifespan & Genetic Concerns: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Long-Term Wellness

Understanding breed-specific health risks helps you anticipate veterinary costs and make informed breeding decisions. Ethical breeders like MFS provide comprehensive health testing to minimize genetic risks, but some conditions remain more prevalent in certain breeds.

Maltipoo Health Profile: Generally Robust with Minor Issues

Common Health Issues in Maltipoos:

  • Patellar Luxation: Kneecap dislocation (common in small breeds). OFA screening identifies at-risk dogs before breeding. Mild cases require no treatment; severe cases need surgery ($1,500-3,000).
  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA): Inherited eye disease leading to blindness. DNA testing eliminates this risk when both parents are clear.
  • White Shaker Syndrome: Neurological tremors in small white dogs. Usually manageable with medication.
  • Dental Disease: Small mouths cause crowding. Daily brushing and annual cleanings prevent issues.
  • Hypoglycemia: Low blood sugar in very small puppies (Teacup/Toy). Managed with frequent meals.

Maltipoo Lifespan: 12-15 years (many reach 16-18 with excellent care)

Lifetime veterinary costs (healthy dog): $300-600 annually for routine care. Unexpected issues add $500-2,000 every few years.

Cockapoo Health Profile: More Serious Concerns

Common Health Issues in Cockapoos:

  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA): THE most significant concern. Cocker Spaniels have high PRA rates—this hereditary eye disease causes gradual blindness. Responsible breeders DNA test, but the condition is widespread in the breed.
  • Chronic Ear Infections: Due to heavy, floppy ears. Expect 2-4 infections per year requiring vet treatment ($150-300 each). Severe cases need ear canal surgery.
  • Hip Dysplasia: More common than in Maltipoos due to Cocker Spaniel structural issues. Can require surgery ($3,000-6,000).
  • Cataracts: Clouding of eye lens, common in Cocker lines. Surgery costs $2,500-4,000 per eye.
  • Seborrhea: Skin condition causing excessive oiliness and odor. Requires medicated shampoos and lifelong management.
  • Autoimmune Disorders: IMHA (Immune-Mediated Hemolytic Anemia) occurs more frequently in Cocker lines. Life-threatening and expensive to treat ($3,000-10,000).
  • Patellar Luxation: Similar risk as Maltipoos.

Cockapoo Lifespan: 12-15 years

Lifetime veterinary costs (healthy dog): $500-900 annually for routine care. Ear infections alone add $400-800/year. If PRA, hip dysplasia, or cataracts develop, expect $5,000-15,000 in treatment costs over the dog’s lifetime.

Which Breed Has Lower Veterinary Costs?

Maltipoos have significantly lower lifetime vet costs because their primary health issues (patellar luxation, PRA) are either preventable through screening or infrequent when parents are properly tested. Most Maltipoos live healthy lives with just routine annual checkups and dental cleanings.

Cockapoos typically incur higher costs due to:

  • Chronic ear infections (budget $400-800/year)
  • Eye problems requiring specialist visits ($300-500/exam)
  • Potential orthopedic issues (hip dysplasia, luxating patellas)

Annual vet costs for Cockapoos often run $1,000-2,000 even without major health crises.

Importance of Health-Tested Parents

When you buy from MFS, all parent Maltipoos undergo:

  • DNA testing for PRA, von Willebrand’s disease, and other genetic conditions
  • OFA patellar luxation screening
  • Annual ophthalmology exams
  • Comprehensive health panels
  • Hip evaluations for breeding dogs over 24 months

For Cockapoo buyers, ALWAYS demand proof of:

  • PRA DNA testing (both parents must be clear or carriers, never affected)
  • OFA hip and elbow certifications
  • Annual eye exams by board-certified veterinary ophthalmologists
  • Ear health verification (parents should have clean ear canals, no chronic infections)

If a Cockapoo breeder cannot provide these results, walk away—you’re buying future health problems.

Training Difficulty & Intelligence: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Ease of Training

Both breeds are intelligent and trainable, but they respond to training differently due to their parent breeds’ working purposes. Understanding these motivational differences helps you set realistic expectations and choose appropriate training methods.

Maltipoo Training: Easy, Eager-to-Please & Beginner-Friendly

Maltipoos combine Poodle intelligence (#2 in Stanley Coren’s rankings) with Maltese compliance, creating a dog that:

  • Learns quickly: Grasps basic commands in 5-10 repetitions
  • Is highly food-motivated: Treat-based training is extremely effective
  • Seeks approval: Responds well to praise and positive reinforcement
  • Rarely stubborn: Wants to please their owner, making corrections easy
  • Housetrains reliably: Most are fully housetrained by 4-6 months with consistent crate training

Training Difficulty Rating: 2/5 (Easy) — Ideal for first-time dog owners

Best Training Methods: Clicker training, short 5-10 minute sessions, positive reinforcement, early socialization starting at 8 weeks.

Cockapoo Training: Moderate Difficulty, Can Be Stubborn

Cockapoos inherit Poodle intelligence but also the Cocker Spaniel’s independent hunting dog mindset, creating a dog that:

  • Is intelligent but selective: Learns quickly but may choose not to obey if unmotivated
  • Has moderate stubbornness: Cocker Spaniels were bred to work independently, so they don’t always look to humans for direction
  • Requires firm consistency: Inconsistent rules lead to confusion and selective obedience
  • Needs high-value rewards: Basic treats may not be motivating enough—use chicken, cheese, or playtime as rewards
  • Housetraining can be challenging: Takes 5-8 months on average due to stubborn streaks

Training Difficulty Rating: 3/5 (Moderate) — Manageable but requires patience and consistency

Best Training Methods: Positive reinforcement with high-value treats, firm (not harsh) corrections, 15-20 minute sessions for focus, professional obedience classes recommended.

Which Breed Is Better for First-Time Owners?

Maltipoo: Far better for beginners. Their eagerness to please and lack of stubbornness mean fewer training frustrations. First-time owners succeed with Maltipoos even without professional training help.

Cockapoo: Better suited for owners with prior dog experience or those willing to invest in professional training classes. Their stubbornness can be mistaken for “not understanding” when in reality they’re choosing not to obey.

Behavioral Issues Comparison

Maltipoo challenges:

  • Separation anxiety (manageable with gradual crate training)
  • Alert barking (trainable with “quiet” commands)
  • Timidity if not socialized (easily prevented)

Cockapoo challenges:

  • Destructive chewing when bored or under-exercised
  • Jumping on people (requires consistent training)
  • Selective hearing during recall (off-leash reliability takes months)
  • Resource guarding (food, toys) if not managed early
  • Excessive barking or whining for attention

Cockapoo behavioral issues almost always stem from insufficient exercise or inconsistent training. Maltipoo issues are typically minor and easily resolved.

Exercise Requirements & Daily Activity Needs: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo Energy Management

This section deserves emphasis because it’s the most underestimated difference between breeds. Many families buy Cockapoos expecting “moderate” exercise needs and discover they’ve adopted an athletic dog requiring daily vigorous activity.

Maltipoo Exercise Needs: Moderate & Manageable

Daily Requirements: 30-45 minutes split between:

  • Two 15-20 minute walks
  • 10-15 minutes indoor play (fetch, tug, puzzle toys)
  • Mental stimulation (training, sniff games)

Maltipoos are “flexible” athletes—they’ll happily join you for longer walks or hikes but don’t require them. They adapt to your energy level. On busy days or bad weather, indoor play and bathroom breaks suffice without behavioral consequences.

Cockapoo Exercise Needs: High & Non-Negotiable

Daily Requirements: 45-60+ minutes of VIGOROUS activity:

  • Two 20-30 minute walks (brisk pace, not leisurely strolls)
  • 20-30 minutes active play (fetch, frisbee, agility, swimming)
  • Mental stimulation (training, puzzle toys, scent work)
  • Weekend long hikes, dog park visits, or swimming sessions highly beneficial

Cockapoos have hunting dog stamina—they don’t tire easily. A quick 15-minute walk won’t satisfy them. Under-exercised Cockapoos develop serious behavioral problems:

  • Destructive chewing (furniture, shoes, walls)
  • Digging (yards, carpet, sofa cushions)
  • Excessive barking or whining
  • Hyperactivity (can’t settle indoors)
  • Escape attempts (jumping fences, darting out doors)

Can You Skip Exercise Sometimes?

Maltipoo: Yes. Missing a walk 1-2 days per week during busy periods, illness, or bad weather is fine. They’ll nap happily.

Cockapoo: No. Skipping even one day of proper exercise creates pent-up energy that manifests as problem behaviors. Cockapoos need daily output—rain, shine, busy schedules, or not.

Apartment vs House: Which Breed Needs a Yard?

Maltipoo: Thrives in apartments. No yard needed. Gets sufficient exercise through leashed walks.

Cockapoo: While they CAN live in apartments, they’re significantly happier with yard access. A fenced yard allows them to run, play fetch, and burn energy between structured walks. Without a yard, Cockapoo owners must commit to 3-4 daily outdoor trips minimum.

If you’re buying a puppy for apartment living, the Maltipoo is the practical, humane choice unless you’re an extremely active person.

Frequently Asked Questions: Maltipoo vs Cockapoo

Q: Which breed is better for families with kids?

A: Cockapoos are better for active families with children who want an outdoor play companion. Their robust size (15-25 lbs) and energetic temperament match active kids perfectly. Maltipoos are better for families with quieter children or limited outdoor space—they’re more delicate and prefer gentler play.

Q: Which breed barks less?

A: Maltipoos bark less overall. They’re alert dogs who will announce visitors but aren’t chronic barkers. Cockapoos can develop excessive barking when bored or under-exercised due to their higher energy levels.

Q: Are Cockapoos healthier than Maltipoos?

A: No. Cockapoos have more serious health concerns, particularly Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA), chronic ear infections, and hip dysplasia inherited from Cocker Spaniels. Maltipoos from health-tested parents typically have fewer long-term health issues and lower veterinary costs.

Q: Which breed is easier to train?

A: Maltipoos are significantly easier to train. They’re eager-to-please and lack the stubbornness common in Cockapoos (inherited from Cocker Spaniels’ independent hunting dog genetics). First-time owners find Maltipoo training straightforward, while Cockapoos often require professional obedience classes.

Q: Can Cockapoos live in apartments?

A: Technically yes, but it’s challenging. Cockapoos need 60+ minutes daily vigorous exercise and access to outdoor spaces for running. Without nearby dog parks or green spaces, apartment-dwelling Cockapoos often develop behavioral problems (destructive chewing, excessive barking). Maltipoos thrive in apartments.

Q: Which breed sheds less?

A: Maltipoos shed less, especially F1B generations (75% Poodle). Cockapoos have denser coats with more undercoat, leading to moderate shedding even in first-generation crosses. For severe allergies, Maltipoo F1B is the reliable choice.

Q: Which breed lives longer?

A: Both typically live 12-15 years. Maltipoos may live slightly longer (16-18 years) because they have fewer serious genetic health issues. Cockapoos’ chronic ear infections and potential eye diseases can impact longevity if not managed properly.

Q: What’s the main reason people regret getting a Cockapoo?

A: Underestimating exercise needs. Many families expect a “moderate energy” dog and discover they’ve adopted an athletic breed requiring 60+ minutes daily activity. When exercise needs aren’t met, Cockapoos develop destructive behaviors. This rarely happens with Maltipoos.
