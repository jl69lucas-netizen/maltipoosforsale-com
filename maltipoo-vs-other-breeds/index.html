Purebred Maltese vs Maltipoo: Understanding the Key Differences

📌 Fast Comparison

If you want the tiniest, purebred, elegant companion with a silky straight coat, the Maltese fits. If you want slightly sturdier size, a hypoallergenic curl, and easier training, the Maltipoo fits better.

🧮 Size & Weight Predictor (Maltese vs Maltipoo)

Purebred Maltese: AKC Lineage & Why Families Compare Them to Maltipoos

The Maltese is one of the oldest companion breeds in the world, recognized by the American Kennel Club (AKC). They are cherished for their refined, silky coat, emotionally intuitive nature, and tiny, portable size. When families compare the Maltipoo vs Maltese, they usually want to determine:

  • ✓ Which dog is better for allergies
  • ✓ Which one is more durable around kids
  • ✓ Which one needs less grooming intensity
  • ✓ Which one is easier to train

Understanding Maltese Temperament Differences

The Maltese grows slowly and stays small throughout life. Their temperament is affectionate, sociable, and gentle, but often more sensitive and delicate than a Maltipoo. Maltese tend to prefer:

  • 🏠 Quiet homes — Less tolerant of loud noise and chaos
  • 👨‍👩‍👧 Adults or older kids — More fragile around toddlers
  • ✂️ Owners who can keep up with coat maintenance — Daily brushing required

Meanwhile, Maltipoos generally adapt to:

  • 🏢 Apartments, suburbs, and small homes — Highly adaptable
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Active families — More patient with children
  • 🐶 New dog owners — Easier training curve
  • 🐾 Multi-pet homes — Better socialization with other animals

This is why many first-time dog owners—whether in California, Texas, or Florida—lean toward the Maltipoo.

Maltese vs Maltipoo Grooming Comparison

A key difference families underestimate is the coat maintenance requirement:

  • 🤍 Purebred Maltese: Straight, silky coat → DAILY brushing required to prevent matting
  • 🌀 Maltipoo: Wavy or curly coat → Brushing every 2–3 days is sufficient

Micro, Teacup & Mini Maltese — Are They Better Than a Maltipoo?

Micro and teacup Maltese are extremely small, often 2–4 pounds, but with small size comes significant health risks:

  • ⚠️ Fragile bones — Prone to fractures from small falls
  • ⚠️ Higher medical risk — Liver shunts, heart defects
  • ⚠️ Blood sugar instability — Hypoglycemia concerns
  • ⚠️ Strict feeding schedules — Must eat every 3-4 hours

Meanwhile, the Maltipoo’s slightly sturdier Poodle genetics reduce fragility without sacrificing small size. Learn why we caution against teacup breeding.

Which Is Better for Kids?

  • 🐶 Maltipoo: Generally more patient, slightly more durable, playful but gentle
  • 🤍 Maltese: Sweet and affectionate, but delicate and often overwhelmed by toddlers

Families with younger children typically choose the Maltipoo for its balanced temperament and sturdier build.

Allergy Comparison — Do Maltese Shed More or Less Than Maltipoos?

Both breeds are considered low-shedding, but there are key differences:

  • 🤍 Maltese: Does not shed, but straight hair drops when brushed, requiring constant grooming
  • 🌀 Maltipoo: Does not shed, with a curl pattern that traps loose hair—ideal for severe allergy sufferers

The Maltipoo Breed: Why This Designer Mix Stands Out

📌 What Makes Maltipoos Special?

The Maltipoo combines the Maltese’s affectionate nature with the Poodle’s intelligence and low-shedding coat, creating an ideal companion for apartments, families, and allergy sufferers.

Understanding Maltipoo Generations: F1, F1B, F2B Explained

Not all Maltipoos are created equal. The generation directly impacts coat type, shedding level, and overall temperament:

  • 🧬 F1 Maltipoo (50% Maltese / 50% Poodle): First-generation cross, moderate shedding, wavy coat
  • 🌀 F1B Maltipoo (25% Maltese / 75% Poodle): Backcross to Poodle, best for severe allergies, curlier coat, virtually no shedding
  • 🔄 F2B Maltipoo: Second-generation backcross, similar to F1B in shedding and coat characteristics

At MFS, we specialize in F1B Maltipoos because they offer the highest level of hypoallergenic qualities while maintaining the sweet Maltese temperament. Read the complete Maltipoo breed guide for detailed genetic breakdowns.

Understanding Hybrid Vigor in Maltipoos

One of the biggest advantages of designer breeds like Maltipoos is hybrid vigor—the phenomenon where crossbred dogs are often healthier than their purebred parents due to increased genetic diversity.

Research shows that purebred dogs have higher rates of:

  • ⚠️ Genetic disorders (due to limited gene pools)
  • ⚠️ Structural issues (from breeding for appearance over health)
  • ⚠️ Immune system weaknesses

Meanwhile, Maltipoos benefit from combining two distinct gene pools, reducing the likelihood of inheriting breed-specific problems from either parent. This is why Maltipoos often have fewer health issues than purebred Maltese or Toy Poodles.

Your Breed Comparison Questions Answered: Comprehensive FAQ

📌 Everything You Need to Know

These are the most common questions families ask when comparing Maltipoos to other breeds. Find detailed, honest answers from 20+ years of breeding experience.

❓ Complete Breed Comparison FAQ

Q: Which breed is truly the most hypoallergenic?

A: F1B Maltipoos (75% Poodle genetics) and purebred Toy Poodles are equally hypoallergenic, producing minimal dander and shedding. F1 Cavapoos and Cockapoos have slightly higher allergen levels due to Cavalier and Cocker Spaniel genetics. If you have severe allergies, choose an F1B Maltipoo or Poodle. Learn more about Maltipoo hypoallergenic qualities.

Q: Are Maltipoos good with young children?

A: Yes, Maltipoos are excellent with children of all ages when properly socialized. They’re more durable than purebred Maltese (less fragile) but gentler than high-energy Cockapoos. Cavapoos are equally good with kids but tend to be calmer. For families with toddlers, we recommend toy or mini Maltipoos (7-15 lbs) over teacup sizes.

Q: Which breed requires the least grooming?

A: Maltipoos require the least maintenance overall—professional grooming every 6-8 weeks and brushing 2-3 times per week. Compare this to Cockapoos (daily brushing needed) and Poodles (professional grooming every 4-6 weeks). Cavapoos fall in the middle. See our complete grooming guide.

Q: Why are Maltipoos less expensive than Cavapoos?

A: MFS Maltipoos are priced fairly at $1,300-$1,700 because we breed for families, not profit. Cavapoos often cost $2,500-$4,500 due to higher demand and the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel’s popularity. Despite lower pricing, our Maltipoos receive the same health testing, socialization, and care as any premium breeder. View our health guarantee.

Q: Do Maltipoos bark more than Cavapoos or Cockapoos?

A: All three breeds have similar barking tendencies—moderate. Cavapoos are slightly quieter due to the Cavalier’s calm nature. Cockapoos may bark more due to the Cocker Spaniel’s alert instincts. Maltipoos fall in the middle and respond well to training. With proper socialization, barking is minimal in all breeds.

Q: Which breed has the fewest health problems?

A: Maltipoos and Cockapoos benefit from hybrid vigor, reducing breed-specific health issues. Cavapoos carry higher genetic risks due to the Cavalier King Charles Spaniel’s predisposition to Mitral Valve Disease (MVD). Purebred Toy Poodles have lower genetic diversity and higher risk of conditions like Addison’s Disease. Health testing is essential regardless of breed. Verify breeder testing through OFA.

Q: Are Maltipoos easier to train than Cockapoos?

A: Slightly yes. Maltipoos have a 2/5 training difficulty (easy), while Cockapoos are 3/5 (moderate). Cavapoos are the easiest at 1.5/5, and purebred Poodles are 1/5 (easiest). Maltipoos are eager to please and respond quickly to positive reinforcement, making them ideal for first-time dog owners.

Q: Can Maltipoos live in apartments as easily as Toy Poodles?

A: Yes, Maltipoos excel in apartments due to their small size (6-12 lbs), moderate energy level, and minimal barking. They require less exercise than Cockapoos and are less anxious than Poodles when left alone for reasonable periods. Families in major cities like New York, Los Angeles, and Chicago consistently choose Maltipoos for apartment living.

Q: How do I know if a breeder is reputable?

A: Reputable breeders provide verifiable health testing (OFA, Embark DNA), offer multi-year health guarantees (not just 1 year), allow you to meet parents or see them via video, and provide references from past buyers. Avoid breeders who won’t show health records, meet in parking lots, or pressure you to buy immediately. Read our complete guide to identifying red flags.

Q: Should I choose a Maltipoo or a Cavapoo for a senior?

A: Cavapoos are slightly better for seniors due to their calmer temperament and lower exercise needs. However, toy Maltipoos (7-10 lbs) are also excellent for seniors who want a portable, affectionate companion. Both breeds form deep bonds and adapt well to quieter households. The main difference: Cavapoos are more laid-back, Maltipoos are more playful.

Preparing for Your New Maltipoo: Lifestyle Tips for Success

📌 Set Your Puppy Up for Success

Bringing home a Maltipoo is exciting! Here’s how to prepare your home, lifestyle, and family for a smooth transition.

Essential Supplies Before Your Puppy Arrives

Stock up on these essentials before delivery day:

  • 🏠 Crate: 24″ x 18″ wire crate with divider (for house training)
  • 🛏️ Bed: Soft, washable bed for crate and sleeping area
  • 🍖 Food: We’ll provide 3 days of food; buy the same brand for consistency
  • 💧 Water/Food Bowls: Stainless steel or ceramic (safer than plastic)
  • 🦴 Toys: Puppy-safe toys for teething and mental stimulation
  • 🚿 Grooming Tools: Slicker brush, comb, nail clippers, puppy shampoo
  • 🏷️ Collar & Leash: Lightweight collar and 4-6 ft leash
  • 🧼 Cleaning Supplies: Enzyme cleaner for accidents

First Week Tips

The first week is crucial for bonding and establishing routines:

  • 📅 Schedule a Vet Visit: Within 3-5 days of arrival for wellness check
  • 🏠 Establish Routines: Feed, potty, and sleep at consistent times
  • 😴 Crate Training: Use crate for naps and nighttime (not punishment)
  • 🚽 Potty Training: Take outside every 2 hours and after meals
  • 🤗 Socialization: Introduce to family members, gentle handling
  • ⚠️ Limit Stress: Avoid overwhelming visitors in the first week

Essential Maltipoo Training Tips for New Owners

📌 Start Training Right

Maltipoos are intelligent and eager to please, making them one of the easiest breeds to train. Here’s how to start on the right paw.

Basic Training Principles for Maltipoos

Maltipoos respond best to positive reinforcement training methods:

  • 🎉 Positive Reinforcement: Reward desired behaviors with treats, praise, and play
  • ⚠️ No Punishment: Never use physical corrections or harsh scolding
  • ⏱️ Consistency: All family members must use the same commands and rules
  • 🕐 Short Sessions: 5-10 minute training sessions, 2-3 times daily
  • 🎯 Start Early: Begin basic commands at 8-10 weeks

Essential Commands to Teach First

Focus on these foundational commands:

  1. “Sit” – Easiest command, foundation for others
  2. “Come” – Critical for safety and recall
  3. “Down” – Promotes calmness
  4. “Stay” – Builds impulse control
  5. “Leave It” – Prevents dangerous situations
  6. Crate Training – Essential for house training and safety
  7. Leash Walking – Prevents pulling and promotes bonding

Read our complete Maltipoo training guide for step-by-step instructions on each command.

Potty Training Timeline

Most Maltipoos are fully house trained by 4-6 months with consistency:

  • 8-10 weeks: Take outside every 1-2 hours, after meals, after naps
  • 10-12 weeks: Begin recognizing cues, fewer accidents
  • 12-16 weeks: Can hold bladder 3-4 hours, fewer accidents
  • 4-6 months: Fully house trained with rare accidents

Tips for success:

  • 🚪 Use the same door and potty spot every time
  • ⏰ Set phone alarms to remind you to take puppy out
  • 🎉 Reward immediately after successful potty outside
  • 🧼 Clean accidents with enzyme cleaner (not ammonia-based)
  • 😌 Be patient—accidents are normal learning experiences

Socialization Windows

Critical socialization period is 8-16 weeks—expose your Maltipoo to:

  • 👥 People: Different ages, genders, appearances
  • 🐾 Other Pets: Vaccinated, friendly dogs and cats
  • 🔊 Sounds: Vacuum, doorbell, traffic, music
  • 🏙️ Environments: Car rides, parks, pet stores (after vaccinations)
  • 🎒 Handling: Paws, ears, mouth, grooming tools

Safety note: Avoid dog parks and public areas until 2 weeks after final puppy vaccinations (usually 16-18 weeks).

Common Maltipoo Behavior Issues

Address these early to prevent bad habits:

  • 😟 Separation Anxiety: Practice leaving for short periods, crate training helps
  • 🗣️ Barking: Teach “Quiet” command, don’t reward attention-seeking barks
  • 🦷 Nipping/Mouthing: Redirect to appropriate toys, stop play if too rough
  • 🚶 Leash Pulling: Stop walking when they pull, resume when leash is loose
