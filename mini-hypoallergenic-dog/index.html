SECTION 4: UNDERSTANDING HYPOALLERGENIC DOGS – EDUCATIONAL SECTION (350 words)

The Science Behind Mini Hypoallergenic Dogs: What Allergy Sufferers Need to Know

CRITICAL TRUTH: No Dog is 100% Hypoallergenic

Before we explain why Teddy is excellent for allergy sufferers, understand this: ALL dogs produce allergens. “Hypoallergenic” means REDUCED allergens, not zero. Teddy produces 80-90% fewer allergens than typical shedding breeds, making him tolerable for most (but not all) allergy sufferers.

What Causes Dog Allergies?

Three Primary Allergens:

1. Dander (Dead Skin Cells) – PRIMARY TRIGGER

  • What: Microscopic skin flakes all dogs shed
  • How it spreads: Attaches to hair, becomes airborne, settles on surfaces
  • Teddy’s Advantage: Healthy skin + non-shedding coat = minimal dander spread

2. Saliva Proteins (Can f 1, Can f 2)

  • What: Proteins in dog saliva, licked onto fur during grooming
  • How it spreads: Dries on fur, becomes airborne particles
  • Teddy’s Advantage: Non-shedding coat means saliva-coated hair stays on dog, not airborne

3. Urine Proteins

  • What: Proteins present in all dog urine
  • How it spreads: Male dogs splash during urination, females less so
  • Teddy’s Management: Proper bathroom habits, outdoor elimination

Why “Hypoallergenic Breeds” Help:

Traditional Shedding Breeds (Labs, Shepherds, Retrievers):

  • Shed hair constantly
  • Dander attached to shed hair becomes airborne
  • Impossible to control allergen spread
  • Result: Continuous allergic reactions

Hypoallergenic Breeds (Poodles, Maltese, Teddy):

  • Don’t shed (or minimal shedding)
  • Dander stays trapped in coat until removed via grooming
  • Controllable allergen exposure
  • Result: 80-90% of allergy sufferers tolerate well

Teddy’s Specific Hypoallergenic Qualities:

Genetic Advantages:

  1. Both Parents Hypoallergenic: Double inheritance of non-shedding genes
  2. F1 Generation: First-generation cross = predictable coat type (50/50 genetics)
  3. Furnishing Gene Positive: Confirmed wavy/wire coat (not straight shedding type)

Physical Assessment:

  1. Skin Health Score: Excellent (week 10 vet evaluation)
  2. Shedding Test: Zero loose hairs during brushing examination
  3. Coat Texture: Wavy, dense (optimal for trapping dander)
  4. Oil Production: Balanced (not dry/flaky which increases dander)

  • Frequency: Once weekly (manageable with Teddy’s 8-15 lb size)
  • Shampoo: Hypoallergenic dog shampoo (removes dander, saliva proteins)
  • Result: Studies show weekly bathing reduces Can f 1 levels 80%
