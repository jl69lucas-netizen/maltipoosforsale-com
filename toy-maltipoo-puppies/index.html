SECTION 21: GROOMING REQUIREMENTS DETAILED

How Much Grooming Do Toy Maltipoos Need?

Toy Maltipoos require moderate grooming to keep their hypoallergenic coats healthy and beautiful. Whether you live in Houston, Texas, St. Louis, Missouri, or Walnut Creek, California, following this grooming schedule will keep ELLA, VICI, TESE, BEND, ALPH, or ATOM looking and feeling their best.

Weekly At-Home Grooming (30-45 minutes total):

Brushing (3-4x Per Week, 10-15 minutes each):

  • Tool: Small slicker brush (Safari or Hertzko)
  • Why: Prevents mats and tangles, removes loose hair
  • How: Brush in direction of hair growth, gently work through any tangles
  • Focus areas: Behind ears, under legs, chest, belly (where mats form)
  • Tip: Make it positive! Give treats, praise throughout

Teeth Brushing (Daily Ideal, 3x Weekly Minimum):

  • Tool: Finger brush or small toothbrush + enzymatic dog toothpaste
  • Why: Prevents dental disease (common in toy breeds)
  • How: Lift lips, brush in circular motions, focus on gum line
  • Duration: 2-3 minutes
  • Never use human toothpaste! (Toxic to dogs)

Ear Cleaning (Weekly):

  • Tool: Ear cleaning solution + cotton balls (never Q-tips!)
  • Why: Prevents infections (floppy ears trap moisture)
  • How: Apply solution to cotton ball, wipe visible ear area gently
  • Signs of infection: Redness, odor, head shaking, scratching

Eye Wiping (Daily):

  • Tool: Warm damp cloth or pet eye wipes
  • Why: Prevents tear staining (common in light-colored Maltipoos)
  • How: Gently wipe from inner corner outward
  • For staining: Use tear stain remover products (Angels’ Eyes, etc.)

Professional Grooming (Every 6-8 Weeks):

What’s Included in Professional Grooming: ✓ Full bath and blow-dry ✓ Haircut/trim to desired style ✓ Nail trimming and filing ✓ Ear cleaning and plucking ✓ Anal gland expression (if needed) ✓ Teeth brushing ✓ Paw pad trimming ✓ Sanitary trim

  • If nervous: Professional groomer can do ($10-15) or vet tech

Bathing (Monthly or As Needed):

  • Frequency: Every 4-6 weeks (or when dirty/smelly)
  • Products: Gentle puppy shampoo (Earthbath, Burt’s Bees)
  • Water temp: Lukewarm (not hot!)
  • Process:
    1. Brush thoroughly BEFORE bath (removes mats)
    2. Wet completely, avoiding eyes and ears
    3. Shampoo, massage, rinse completely
    4. Condition if desired (helps detangling)
    5. Towel dry, then blow-dry on low/cool setting
    6. Brush again while drying

Never over-bathe! Strips natural oils, causes dry skin.

Age-Appropriate Feeding Schedule:

10 Weeks to 6 Months (Current Age Range):

Frequency: 4 TIMES DAILY (Critical!)

  • Breakfast: 7-8 AM
  • Lunch: 11 AM-12 PM
  • Dinner: 4-5 PM
  • Bedtime Snack: 9-10 PM

Portion Size: 1/4 to 1/3 cup per meal (Total: 1 to 1.5 cups daily) Calories: ~250-350 calories per day

Why 4x Daily? Toy breeds have fast metabolisms and small stomachs. Going more than 5-6 hours without food can cause dangerous hypoglycemia (low blood sugar).

Preventing Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar):

Critical for Toy Breeds Under 6 Months!

Warning Signs: 🚨 Lethargy, weakness 🚨 Wobbling, disorientation 🚨 Trembling, shivering 🚨 Glazed eyes 🚨 Seizures (severe cases)

Prevention: ✓ Feed 4x daily until 6 months (NEVER skip meals!) ✓ Keep NutriCal or Karo syrup on hand ✓ Avoid stress and overexertion ✓ Monitor energy levels ✓ Don’t let puppy get too cold

Emergency Treatment:

  1. Rub NutriCal/Karo syrup on gums immediately
  2. Offer food if conscious and able to eat
  3. Keep warm
  4. Call vet if symptoms don’t improve in 15 minutes
  5. Call us 24/7: (531) 368-0538

This is 100% preventable with proper feeding!
