🐕 General Maltipoo Questions

What exactly is a Maltipoo? Is it a purebred dog?

+

A Maltipoo is a designer hybrid breed created by crossing a purebred Maltese with a purebred Toy or Miniature Poodle. They are not purebred—they’re a first-generation (F1) cross between two distinct purebred parents.

This intentional crossbreeding combines the best traits of both breeds: the Maltese’s gentle, affectionate nature with the Poodle’s intelligence and hypoallergenic coat. The result is a small, smart, low-shedding companion perfect for Indianapolis apartments and Indiana families with allergies.

While not recognized by the AKC as a standalone breed (yet), Maltipoos are one of the most popular designer dogs in America. At MFS, both parent dogs are AKC-registered purebreds with documented pedigrees, ensuring your Indiana Maltipoo comes from quality lineage.

How big will my Maltipoo get? Can you guarantee adult size?

Are Maltipoos really hypoallergenic? Will they trigger my allergies?

+

Maltipoos are considered hypoallergenic but with an important caveat: no dog is 100% allergen-free. People are allergic to dander (dead skin cells) and saliva proteins—not fur itself. Here’s what makes Maltipoos better for Indiana allergy sufferers:

  • Minimal shedding: Poodle genetics = hair that grows continuously (like humans) instead of shedding seasonally
  • Less dander dispersal: Because they shed minimally, dander stays on the dog vs floating around your Indianapolis home
  • Single coat: No undercoat = less allergen production

Real-world results from Indiana families: About 80% of our customers with mild-moderate allergies report little to no allergic reactions. Those with severe allergies should visit our facility or request a “test visit” with one of our parent dogs before committing.

Pro tip for Fort Wayne allergy sufferers: Regular grooming (every 6-8 weeks), weekly brushing, and bathing reduces dander significantly. We also recommend HEPA air filters in bedrooms—this combo works wonders for most families!

+

Maltipoos typically live 12-15 years, with many reaching 16-18 years with proper care! Small breed dogs generally outlive larger breeds, and the Maltipoo’s hybrid vigor (genetic diversity from crossing two breeds) often contributes to longevity.

  • Quality diet: Premium food = healthier organs and joints
  • Regular vet care: Yearly checkups catch issues early (we provide Indiana vet referrals)
  • Dental hygiene: Brush teeth 3x/week—dental disease shortens lifespan
  • Healthy weight: Obesity is the #1 preventable health issue
  • Mental stimulation: Training and play keep minds sharp

With our 10-Year Health Guarantee and comprehensive Puppy Culture early development, your Indianapolis Maltipoo is set up for the longest, healthiest life possible. We’ve had customers whose MFS Maltipoos lived to 17+ years!

🌦️ Year-Round Indiana Maltipoo Care Guide

Indiana’s four-season climate requires different care strategies throughout the year. Here’s how to keep your Maltipoo healthy and happy through Indianapolis winters, spring storms, summer humidity, and beautiful Hoosier autumns.

❄️

Winter Care

December – February • 20-40°F

🧥 Cold Weather Protection:

  • Below 40°F: Light sweater for 15+ min walks
  • Below 30°F: Winter coat required
  • Below 20°F: Coat + booties mandatory
  • Below 10°F: Limit outdoor time to 5-10 min
  • Polar vortex days: Indoor potty pads only

👢 Indianapolis Sidewalk Safety:

  • Salt dangers: Wipe paws after EVERY walk
  • Ice balls: Check between toe pads
  • Booties help: Protect from salt & cold
  • Paw balm: Apply before walks (Musher’s Secret)

🏠 Indoor Winter Care:

  • Dry air: Use humidifier (prevents dry skin)
  • Heater safety: Keep Maltipoo away from vents
  • Snow play: Yes! But dry off immediately after
  • Calories: May need 10% more food (cold = energy)

🌸

Spring Care

March – May • 45-70°F

🌼 Allergy Season in Indiana:

  • Pollen high: Wipe paws & coat after walks
  • Itchy skin? Could be seasonal allergies
  • Allergy symptoms: Excessive licking, red eyes
  • Fort Wayne spring: Tree pollen peaks April
  • Vet visit if: Constant scratching/licking

⛈️ Indiana Storm Safety:

  • Tornado season: Create safe room plan
  • Thunder anxiety: ThunderShirt or calming treats
  • Never leave outside: During severe weather
  • ID tags critical: In case of separation

🦟 Parasite Prevention:

  • Start preventatives: March 1st
  • Heartworm: Monthly pill (year-round best)
  • Flea/tick: Topical or oral monthly
  • Ticks common: In Evansville wooded areas

☀️

Summer Care

June – August • 75-90°F + Humidity

🌡️ Heat Safety (Indiana Humidity!):

  • Walk times: Before 9am or after 7pm only
  • Pavement test: 5-second hand test (too hot?)
  • Never in cars: Fatal within 10 minutes
  • Water always: Fresh water every 2-3 hours
  • Shade required: If outside longer than 15 min

💧 Dehydration Prevention:

  • Warning signs: Dry gums, lethargy, sunken eyes
  • Water everywhere: Multiple bowls around home
  • Portable water: Bring on walks/car trips
  • Ice cubes: Great summer treats!

🏊 Indiana Lake Safety:

  • Can Maltipoos swim? Some yes, some no
  • Life jacket: Required if near water
  • Blue-green algae: Toxic! Check for warnings
  • Rinse after: Lake water can irritate skin

🍂

Fall Care

September – November • 45-70°F

🍁 Beautiful Indiana Autumn:

  • Perfect weather: Best walking season!
  • Brown County trips: Maltipoos love leaf trails
  • Allergy season 2: Ragweed peaks September
  • Exercise boost: Great time for longer walks

🎃 Halloween Safety:

  • Chocolate dangers: Keep candy away!
  • Costumes: Make sure they’re comfortable
  • Doorbell stress: Create quiet safe space
  • Xylitol alert: In some candies – toxic!

🦃 Thanksgiving Hazards:

  • No turkey bones: Splinter = emergency
  • No onions/garlic: Toxic to dogs
  • Plain turkey OK: Small pieces, no skin
  • Visitor stress: Provide quiet retreat

  • Annual wellness exam
  • Heartworm test (spring)
  • Dental cleaning (as needed)
  • Vaccine boosters (per vet)
  • Senior checkups (7+ years = twice yearly)
