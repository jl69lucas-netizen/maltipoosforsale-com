Frequently Asked Questions About Maltipoo Puppies in Florida

We’ve answered thousands of questions from Florida families over the past 20 years. Here are the most common:

❓ Are Maltipoos good for Florida’s hot climate?

Answer: Yes! Maltipoos adapt very well to Florida’s warm weather. Their small size helps them regulate body temperature, and their coat provides sun protection. However, Maltipoos should live primarily indoors with air conditioning during hot summer months. Schedule walks for early morning or evening when temperatures are cooler, provide constant access to fresh water, and never leave them outdoors in peak heat. Many Florida Maltipoo owners opt for shorter “summer cuts” to help their pups stay comfortable.

❓ How much does it cost to fly a Maltipoo to Florida?

❓ Are Maltipoos hypoallergenic and non-shedding?

Answer: Maltipoos are considered hypoallergenic and low-shedding, making them excellent choices for Florida allergy sufferers. Thanks to their Poodle genetics, Maltipoos produce significantly less dander than most breeds. While no dog is 100% hypoallergenic (allergies react to dander, saliva, and urine, not just fur), Maltipoos are among the best breeds for people with dog allergies. Florida’s high pollen counts can make allergies worse, so having a low-allergen dog is especially valuable. We recommend spending time with the puppy (video calls available) before committing if allergies are a severe concern.

❓ How big will my Maltipoo get?

Answer: Adult Maltipoo size depends on the classification:

  • Teacup Maltipoos: 4-6 lbs fully grown (smallest size)
  • Toy Maltipoos: 6-10 lbs fully grown (most popular)
  • Mini Maltipoos: 10-15 lbs fully grown (larger size)

Size is influenced by parent dogs—Toy Poodle parents produce smaller puppies while Miniature Poodle parents create larger Maltipoos. We can provide estimated adult size based on parents’ weights and the puppy’s growth trajectory. Most Maltipoos reach full adult size by 8-12 months old. Florida’s climate doesn’t affect size, but proper nutrition does!

❓ How long do Maltipoos live?

Answer: Maltipoos typically live 12-15 years, with many reaching 16-18 years with excellent care. Small breeds generally live longer than large breeds, and Maltipoos benefit from hybrid vigor (increased genetic diversity from crossbreeding). Factors affecting lifespan include genetics (why health testing matters!), diet, exercise, preventive veterinary care, dental health, and weight management. Florida’s warm climate allows year-round outdoor activity, which contributes to longevity. MFS’s comprehensive health testing and Puppy Culture protocols give your Maltipoo the best start for a long, healthy life.

❓ Are Maltipoos easy to train?

Answer: Yes! Maltipoos are highly intelligent (thanks to Poodle genetics) and eager to please (Maltese trait), making them one of the easiest small breeds to train. Our Puppy Culture protocols give them a significant head start—many Florida families report their MFS Maltipoos potty trained within 1-2 weeks. Maltipoos excel at obedience training, tricks, and even agility courses. They respond best to positive reinforcement methods (treats, praise, play). Consistency is key, and Florida’s many professional trainers and puppy classes provide excellent support. We provide detailed training guides specific to Florida environments (dealing with wildlife, beach socialization, etc.).

❓ Can Maltipoos go to Florida beaches?

Answer: Absolutely! Many Florida beaches have dog-friendly areas, and Maltipoos typically love beach outings. Important considerations: check beach rules (some beaches restrict dogs to certain hours or seasons), apply dog-safe sunscreen to light-colored coats, provide fresh water (salt water causes dehydration), rinse coat and paws after swimming, watch for hot sand (can burn paws), avoid rough surf (Maltipoos are small and waves can be dangerous), monitor for jellyfish, and keep on leash in designated areas. Popular dog-friendly Florida beaches include Fort De Soto (voted America’s best dog beach!), Hollywood Beach, Lido Beach, and Atlantic Beach. Your Maltipoo will love the sand and social experience!

❓ Do Maltipoos get along with children?

Answer: Yes! Maltipoos are excellent family dogs and generally wonderful with children. They’re playful, gentle, and patient—perfect qualities for kid-friendly pets. However, due to their small size, supervision is important with very young children (under 5 years) to prevent accidental injuries. Teach children to be gentle, avoid rough play, never pick up puppy by legs or tail, respect puppy’s space when eating or sleeping, and approach calmly. Maltipoos raised with Puppy Culture (like all MFS puppies) have enhanced socialization with children of various ages, making them even better family companions. ❓ How do I prepare my Florida home for a Maltipoo puppy?

Answer: Puppy-Proofing Checklist for Florida Homes:

  • Indoor: Secure electrical cords, remove toxic plants (common in Florida: oleander, sago palms), block off stairs initially, set up crate and pen area, designate potty training spot, remove small objects puppy could swallow
  • Outdoor: Check for gaps in fencing (Florida wildlife!), remove poisonous plants, create shaded areas, ensure pool is fenced/covered, eliminate standing water (mosquitoes), inspect for fire ant mounds
  • Supplies Needed: Quality puppy food (we recommend specific brands), stainless steel bowls, crate and bed, puppy pads, collar and leash, ID tag, toys, grooming supplies, first aid kit
  • Florida-Specific: Cooling mat for hot days, paw protection balm for hot surfaces, sunscreen for dogs, heartworm preventive, flea/tick prevention
