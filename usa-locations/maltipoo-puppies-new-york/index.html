Everything You Need to Know About Maltipoo Puppies

The Maltipoo is a designer dog breed created by crossing a purebred Maltese with a purebred Toy or Miniature Poodle. First bred in the United States during the 1990s, Maltipoos quickly became one of the most sought-after hybrid breeds due to their adorable looks, hypoallergenic coats, and loving personalities.

Physical Characteristics

Size Options:

  • Teacup Maltipoos: 4-7 pounds, 8-10 inches tall
  • Toy Maltipoos: 7-12 pounds, 10-12 inches tall
  • Mini Maltipoos: 12-15 pounds, 12-14 inches tall

Coat & Colors:
Maltipoos inherit the best of both parent breeds. Their coats are typically soft, wavy or curly, and low-shedding—making them ideal for allergy sufferers in New York. Common colors include:

  • Cream & white
  • Apricot & caramel
  • Brown & chocolate
  • Silver & gray
  • Parti-color combinations

Grooming Needs:
Maltipoos require regular grooming to prevent matting. We recommend:

  • Brushing 3-4 times per week

  • Regular nail trims and ear cleaning

Preparing Your Maltipoo for New York’s Four-Season Climate

New York experiences dramatic seasonal variation, from hot, humid summers to cold, snowy winters. Fortunately, Maltipoos adapt wonderfully to New York’s climate with proper care and preparation.

Summer Care (June – August)

Temperature Range: 70°F – 90°F
Humidity: Often high, especially in NYC

Summer Tips:

  • Hydration: Always carry a collapsible water bowl on walks
  • Paw Protection: Hot pavement can burn sensitive paws. Walk during cooler morning/evening hours or use dog booties
  • Air Conditioning: Maltipoos are comfortable indoors with AC. Limit outdoor time during heat waves
  • Grooming: Consider a shorter summer cut for comfort (but not too short—they still need some coat protection)
  • Beach Safety: If visiting dog-friendly beaches, provide shade and fresh water

NYC Summer Alert: Sidewalks and asphalt reach extreme temperatures. The “five-second rule” helps: if you can’t hold your hand on the pavement for five seconds, it’s too hot for your dog’s paws.

Fall Care (September – November)

Temperature Range: 45°F – 70°F

Fall Tips:

  • Perfect Weather: Fall is ideal for longer walks and outdoor adventures
  • Leaf Pile Safety: While fun, check leaf piles for hidden hazards before your Maltipoo jumps in
  • Tick Prevention: Fall remains tick season in NY. Use veterinarian-recommended prevention
  • Allergy Awareness: Some Maltipoos may be sensitive to ragweed and mold. Wipe paws after walks

Winter Care (December – March)

Temperature Range: 20°F – 40°F
Snow/Ice: Common, especially upstate

Winter Tips:

  • Sweaters & Coats: Maltipoos have minimal body fat and single coats. A dog sweater or jacket is essential for extended outdoor time below 40°F
  • Paw Care: Ice, snow, and salt can irritate paws. Use dog booties or apply paw balm before walks. Rinse paws after walks to remove salt and chemicals
  • Indoor Exercise: Provide indoor play and enrichment activities during extreme cold
  • Shorter Walks: Limit outdoor time to 15-20 minutes in sub-freezing temperatures
  • Dry Thoroughly: Always dry your Maltipoo after snow or rain exposure

Buffalo/Rochester Winters: Upstate New York sees significant snowfall. Invest in quality winter gear for your Maltipoo and clear designated potty areas in your yard.

Spring Care (April – May)

Temperature Range: 45°F – 70°F

Spring Tips:

  • Allergy Season: Pollen levels soar. Watch for excessive scratching, eye discharge, or paw licking
  • Mud Season: Particularly relevant upstate. Keep grooming wipes handy for muddy paws
  • Spring Shedding: Even low-shedding Maltipoos may shed slightly in spring. Increase brushing frequency
  • Tick & Flea Prevention: Start preventive treatments early in spring

Year-Round New York Considerations

Air Quality: NYC and urban areas may have poor air quality days. Limit outdoor activity when AQI is high.

Training Your Maltipoo for Success in New York

Proper training is essential for any dog, but it’s especially important in New York’s densely populated areas where your Maltipoo will encounter countless people, dogs, sounds, and situations. At MFS, we give your puppy a head start with ENS and Puppy Culture protocols, but continued training at home ensures long-term success.

Essential Training for NY Maltipoos

1. Socialization
Expose your Maltipoo to:

  • Different people (ages, appearances, voices)
  • Other dogs (various sizes and breeds)
  • Urban sounds (sirens, traffic, construction)
  • Public transit (if relevant)
  • Various surfaces (grates, elevators, stairs)

Best NYC Socialization Spots:

  • Central Park – Weekend mornings for controlled dog meetings
  • Union Square – People-watching and urban sounds
  • Dog-Friendly Cafes – Practice calm behavior around distractions

2. Leash Training
New York requires leashes in public spaces. Train your Maltipoo to:

  • Walk politely without pulling
  • Stop and sit at curbs before crossing streets
  • Ignore distractions (pigeons, squirrels, other dogs)
  • Respond to “heel” and “leave it” commands

3. Crate Training
Crucial for apartment living and vet visits. A properly crate-trained Maltipoo:

  • Has a safe personal space
  • Is easier to manage during repairs or visitors
  • Travels safely
  • Prevents destructive behavior when alone

4. House Training

Understanding New York Dog Ownership Regulations

New York has specific laws and regulations designed to protect pets and the community. Understanding these requirements ensures you’re a responsible, compliant dog owner.

NYC Dog Licensing Requirements

All dogs over 4 months old living in New York City must be licensed through the NYC Department of Health and Mental Hygiene.

How to License Your Dog:

  1. Visit the NYC Health Department website or call 311
  2. Provide proof of current rabies vaccination
  3. Pay the licensing fee:
  • Spayed/Neutered: $8.50
  • Unspayed/Unneutered: $34

Renew annually and display the license tag on your dog’s collar at all times when in public.

Penalties: Failure to license your dog can result in fines up to $200.

New York State Rabies Vaccination Law

New York Public Health Law Section 2141 requires:

  • All dogs must be vaccinated against rabies by 4 months of age
  • Rabies vaccinations must be kept current (typically every 1-3 years depending on vaccine type)
  • Proof of vaccination must be carried when your dog is in public

MFS puppies receive their first vaccinations before delivery, and we provide complete veterinary records. You’ll need to continue the vaccination schedule with your New York veterinarian.

Leash Laws

New York City:

  • Dogs must be on a leash no longer than 6 feet in public spaces

Upstate New York:

  • Leash laws vary by municipality
  • State parks typically require leashes
  • Check local ordinances for your specific city/town

Waste Removal (“Pooper Scooper Law”)

Housing Regulations

Under New York law and the Fair Housing Act, landlords must provide reasonable accommodations for ESAs with proper documentation from a licensed mental health professional. However, ESAs do NOT have the same public access rights as service animals.

Socialization Tips for Success

4. Avoid Forced Interactions:
If your puppy is fearful, don’t force them to approach. Allow them to observe from a distance and approach at their own pace.

5. End on a Positive Note:
Always conclude socialization sessions with a success, even if small.

Final FAQs About Maltipoos in New York

Are Maltipoos good for people with allergies?

Yes! Maltipoos are among the best breeds for allergy sufferers. Their Poodle genetics result in low-shedding, hypoallergenic coats that produce minimal dander. While no dog is 100% hypoallergenic, Maltipoos are as close as you can get. Many of our New York customers specifically chose Maltipoos due to family allergies and report excellent results.

How do Maltipoos do in extreme weather?

Hot Weather (NYC Summers):
Maltipoos handle heat moderately well but need protection:

  • Avoid midday walks on hot pavement
  • Provide plenty of water and shade
  • Keep indoor spaces air-conditioned
  • Watch for signs of overheating (excessive panting, lethargy)

Cold Weather (NY Winters):
Small dogs like Maltipoos have minimal body fat and need protection in cold temperatures:

  • Use dog sweaters/coats below 40°F
  • Protect paws with booties or balm
  • Limit outdoor time in freezing temperatures
  • Dry thoroughly after snow exposure

With proper preparation, Maltipoos thrive in all four New York seasons!

Can Maltipoos do agility or dog sports?

Absolutely! While they’re small, Maltipoos are athletic and intelligent. Many excel in:

  • Agility courses
  • Rally obedience
  • Trick training
  • Nose work
  • Therapy dog work

NYC Agility Training:

  • Zoom Room (Manhattan, Brooklyn, Albany)
  • Canine Country Club (Brooklyn)

Do Maltipoos shed seasonally?

Maltipoos are non-shedding to minimal shedding. Unlike double-coated breeds that “blow their coat” twice a year, Maltipoos have a single coat that sheds very little year-round. You might notice slightly more loose hair during spring and fall, but nothing compared to traditional shedding breeds.

Are Maltipoos yappy or barky?

No! One of the best qualities of Maltipoos is their relatively quiet nature. Unlike some small breeds (like Chihuahuas or Yorkies) that bark excessively, Maltipoos are typically calm and only bark to alert you to visitors or unusual sounds. This makes them excellent for apartment living where excessive noise would disturb neighbors. Proper training further ensures quiet behavior.

Can my Maltipoo eat human food?

Some human foods are safe in moderation:

  • Plain chicken, turkey, beef (cooked, no bones)
  • Carrots, green beans, sweet potato
  • Blueberries, apples (no seeds), bananas
  • Plain rice and oatmeal
  • Peanut butter (xylitol-free only!)
  • Plain yogurt (small amounts)

Toxic Foods to Never Give:

  • Chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic
  • Xylitol (artificial sweetener)
  • Avocado, macadamia nuts, alcohol

Best Practice: Stick primarily to high-quality dog food and use human food only as occasional treats (no more than 10% of diet).

How often should I bathe my Maltipoo?

Every 3-4 weeks is ideal for Maltipoos. Bathing too frequently can strip natural oils and dry out skin, while bathing too infrequently allows dirt and oils to build up.

NYC Considerations:

  • Urban environments may require more frequent bathing due to pollution and sidewalk grime
  • Use gentle, dog-specific shampoos
  • Always brush thoroughly before bathing to prevent matting

Do Maltipoos have a favorite person in the family?

Maltipoos can develop a slight preference for one person, but they typically love all family members. To prevent over-bonding with one person:

  • Have different family members handle feeding, walking, and playtime
  • Encourage varied interactions
  • Avoid having one person do all the caregiving

Can I travel with my Maltipoo on planes?

Yes! Maltipoos are excellent travel companions due to their small size. They can fly in-cabin in an airline-approved carrier (typically under 20 lbs, including carrier). Tips for air travel:

  • Fly non-stop when possible
  • Check airline-specific requirements

  • Some airlines require health certificates for travel
