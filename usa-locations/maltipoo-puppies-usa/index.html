Before bringing home any puppy, understanding the breed is essential. Maltipoos have unique characteristics that make them one of America’s most beloved designer breeds—but are they the right fit for your family?

What is a Maltipoo? Understanding This Beloved Designer Breed

A Maltipoo is a designer dog breed—a intentional cross between a purebred Maltese and a purebred Toy or Miniature Poodle. This isn’t an accidental mix; it’s a carefully planned breeding program designed to combine the best traits of both parent breeds.

The Maltese Contribution:

  • Gentle, affectionate temperament
  • Long history as companion dogs (over 2,000 years)
  • Small size and manageable exercise needs
  • Playful, sweet personality
  • Loyal, devoted nature

The Poodle Contribution:

  • High intelligence (ranked #2 most intelligent breed)
  • Hypoallergenic, low-shedding coat
  • Athletic ability and trainability
  • Robust genetic diversity
  • Longer lifespan potential

When you combine these parent breeds, you get a Maltipoo—a small, intelligent, affectionate companion with a low-shedding coat and an eager-to-please personality. The goal of Maltipoo breeding is to maximize the positive traits of both breeds while minimizing breed-specific health concerns through hybrid vigor.

Designer Breed vs. Purebred: What’s the Difference?

Unlike purebred dogs recognized by the American Kennel Club (AKC), Maltipoos are not an official breed. However, they are recognized by:

  • American Canine Hybrid Club (ACHC)
  • Designer Breed Registry (DBR)
  • International Designer Canine Registry (IDCR)

The advantage of a well-bred designer dog is hybrid vigor—the tendency for crossbred animals to exhibit better health and vitality than purebred parents due to increased genetic diversity. This is why responsible Maltipoo breeders (like MFS) carefully select parent dogs from diverse, health-tested lines.

Are Maltipoos Really Hypoallergenic and Non-Shedding?

This is one of the most common questions we receive, and the answer deserves some nuance.

The Reality of “Hypoallergenic”:
No dog breed is 100% hypoallergenic. All dogs produce some level of allergens through dander (dead skin cells), saliva, and urine. However, Maltipoos are considered low-allergenic or hypoallergenic-friendly because:

  1. Poodle Genetics: Poodles have hair rather than fur, which traps dander close to the body instead of releasing it into the air
  2. Minimal Shedding: Both Maltese and Poodles are low-shedding breeds
  3. Less Dander Distribution: Hair-type coats don’t disperse allergens throughout your home as much as fur-type coats

What This Means for Allergy Sufferers:
Many people with mild to moderate dog allergies can successfully live with Maltipoos. However, we always recommend:

  • Spending time with Maltipoos before committing (visit our facility or arrange a meet-and-greet)
  • Consulting with your allergist
  • Understanding that individual reactions vary
  • Implementing good grooming practices (regular bathing and brushing reduces dander)

Shedding Characteristics:
Maltipoos are minimal shedders compared to most breeds. You won’t find clouds of dog hair on your furniture or clothing. However, they do require regular grooming to prevent matting:

  • Brushing: 3-4 times per week (daily for longer coats)
  • Professional Grooming: Every 6-8 weeks
  • Bathing: Every 3-4 weeks

Think of Maltipoo coat maintenance similar to maintaining your own hair—regular care keeps it healthy, tangle-free, and looking great.

How Do You Housebreak a Maltipoo Puppy?

The MFS Potty Training Method:

Week 1-2 (Establishment):

  1. Take puppy outside every 1-2 hours
  2. Always after eating, drinking, playing, or waking up
  3. Choose a specific “potty spot” and use it consistently
  4. Use verbal cue (“Go potty” or “Do your business”)
  5. Immediately reward successful outdoor potties with treats and praise

Week 3-4 (Pattern Recognition):

  1. Extend time between breaks to 2-3 hours
  2. Watch for “I need to go” signals (sniffing, circling, whining)
  3. Continue consistent rewards
  4. Begin adding verbal cue before puppy goes

Week 5-8 (Independence Building):

  1. Puppy should start signaling when they need to go out
  2. Consider bell training (puppy rings bell to go out)
  3. Extend to 3-4 hours between breaks
  4. Gradually reduce treat frequency (but maintain praise)

3-4 Months (Near Completion):

  • Most puppies can hold it 4-6 hours
  • Fewer accidents
  • Reliable signaling
  • Nighttime control developing

Expected Timeline: Most Maltipoos are reliably housebroken by 4-5 months with consistent training.

Accident Management:

  • Never punish accidents (causes fear and confusion)
  • Clean with enzymatic cleaner to remove odor
  • Increase supervision and frequency of breaks
  • Consider whether medical issues might be involved

What Are Common Health Issues and How to Prevent Them?

Dental Disease (Most Common):

  • Prevention: Brush teeth 3-4 times weekly, dental chews, annual cleanings
  • Signs: Bad breath, discolored teeth, reluctance to eat
  • Importance: Small breeds are highly prone to dental issues

Ear Infections:

  • Prevention: Weekly cleaning, keep ears dry after baths
  • Signs: Head shaking, scratching ears, odor, redness
  • Note: Floppy ears trap moisture, creating infection risk

Obesity:

  • Prevention: Measure food, regular exercise, limited treats
  • Signs: Can’t feel ribs easily, loss of waist definition, decreased energy
  • Impact: Leads to joint problems, heart issues, diabetes

Tear Staining:

  • Prevention: Daily face wiping, quality diet, eye supplements if needed
  • Signs: Brown/red staining under eyes
  • Note: More common in light-colored Maltipoos

Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar – in teacup puppies):

  • Prevention: Feed frequent small meals, monitor energy
  • Signs: Lethargy, shakiness, confusion, weakness
  • Action: Offer honey or Karo syrup immediately, contact vet

General Maltipoo Questions

Q: Are Maltipoos good for first-time dog owners?
A: Absolutely! Maltipoos are one of the best breeds for first-time owners due to their trainability, manageable size, adaptable temperament, and moderate exercise needs. They’re forgiving of beginner mistakes and eager to please.

Q: How long do Maltipoos live?
A: Well-bred Maltipoos typically live 12-15 years, with many reaching 15-17 years. Small breeds generally live longer than large breeds, and hybrid vigor from crossing two breeds often contributes to longevity.

Q: Do Maltipoos bark a lot?
A: Maltipoos are generally low to moderate barkers. They may alert bark when someone comes to the door, but they’re not typically nuisance barkers. Early training can minimize unwanted barking.

Q: Are Maltipoos hypoallergenic?
A: While no dog is 100% hypoallergenic, Maltipoos are considered highly allergy-friendly due to their Poodle genetics and minimal shedding. Many people with dog allergies can tolerate Maltipoos well. We always recommend spending time with Maltipoos before committing if you have allergies.

Q: What’s the difference between F1, F1B, and F2 Maltipoos?
A:

  • F1 Maltipoo: First generation (Maltese + Poodle) – 50% Maltese, 50% Poodle
  • F1B Maltipoo: Backcross generation (F1 Maltipoo + Poodle) – 25% Maltese, 75% Poodle (curlier coat, more hypoallergenic)
  • F2 Maltipoo: Second generation (F1 Maltipoo + F1 Maltipoo) – 50% Maltese, 50% Poodle (more genetic variation)

At MFS, we primarily breed F1 Maltipoos for optimal hybrid vigor and consistent traits.
