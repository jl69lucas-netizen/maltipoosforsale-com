Everything Washington Families Need to Know About the Maltipoo Breed

Before bringing any puppy into your Washington home, understanding the breed’s characteristics, needs, and personality is essential. Maltipoos are one of America’s most beloved designer breeds, and for good reason—but they’re not the right fit for every family.

What is a Maltipoo? Understanding This Beloved Designer Breed

A Maltipoo is a designer crossbreed created by breeding a purebred Maltese with a purebred Toy or Miniature Poodle. This intentional crossing combines the Maltese’s affectionate, gentle nature with the Poodle’s intelligence, hypoallergenic coat, and trainability.

Unlike “accidental” mixed breeds, Maltipoos are purposefully bred to create specific traits:

  • Hypoallergenic coat: Inherited from Poodle genetics, reducing allergic reactions
  • Minimal shedding: Perfect for Washington families who don’t want dog hair everywhere
  • Small size: Ideal for apartments, condos, and smaller living spaces throughout Seattle, Tacoma, and Spokane
  • Affectionate temperament: Combining the Maltese’s devotion with the Poodle’s playfulness
  • High intelligence: Poodles rank as the 2nd smartest dog breed, making Maltipoos highly trainable

Designer Breed vs. Purebred: What’s the Difference?

Designer breeds like Maltipoos are first-generation (F1) crosses between two purebred dogs. This practice, called hybrid vigor or heterosis, often results in healthier puppies with fewer genetic health issues than either parent breed. By introducing genetic diversity, responsible breeders can reduce the incidence of breed-specific health problems while maintaining desirable traits.

Purebred dogs come from a closed gene pool with generations of dogs from the same breed. While this creates predictability in appearance and temperament, it can also concentrate genetic health issues.

At MFS, we embrace the best of both worlds: We use health-tested, AKC-registered purebred Maltese and Poodles to create Maltipoo puppies that benefit from hybrid vigor while maintaining the beautiful appearance and wonderful temperament both breeds are known for.

Are Maltipoos Really Hypoallergenic and Non-Shedding?

This is one of the most common questions Washington families ask, and the answer requires nuance. No dog is 100% hypoallergenic, as allergies are triggered by proteins in dander, saliva, and urine—not just fur. However, Maltipoos are among the best breeds for allergy sufferers.

Here’s why Maltipoos are considered hypoallergenic:

  • Minimal Shedding: Maltipoos inherit the Poodle’s non-shedding coat. While they do lose some hair (all mammals do), they don’t experience the seasonal shedding of breeds like Golden Retrievers or German Shepherds. You won’t find tumbleweeds of dog hair around your Seattle home.
  • Single-Layer Coat: Unlike double-coated breeds that shed undercoat seasonally, Maltipoos have hair (not fur) that grows continuously, similar to human hair. This means less dander released into your Washington home’s environment.
  • Regular Grooming Helps: Consistent grooming (every 4-6 weeks) removes dead hair and dander before it becomes airborne, further reducing allergen exposure.

For Washington allergy sufferers: We recommend spending time around Maltipoos before committing to ensure compatibility. Why Is Embark DNA Testing Critical for Your Washington Maltipoo’s Health?

Not all health testing is created equal. MFS uses Embark DNA testing—the most comprehensive canine genetic screening available—on every parent dog in our breeding program. Here’s why this matters to Washington families:

What Is Embark DNA Testing?

Embark is the gold standard in canine genetic testing, developed by Cornell University veterinary geneticists. A simple cheek swab provides:

  • ✅ Screening for 200+ genetic health conditions
  • ✅ Breed identification and ancestry analysis
  • ✅ Trait predictions (coat type, size, color)
  • ✅ COI (Coefficient of Inbreeding) score
  • ✅ Genetic diversity analysis

What Conditions Does Embark Screen For in Maltese and Poodles?

Key genetic conditions that can affect Maltipoos:

  • Progressive Retinal Atrophy (PRA): Inherited blindness
  • Von Willebrand Disease: Blood clotting disorder
  • Hyperuricosuria (HUU): Bladder/kidney stones
  • Degenerative Myelopathy: Spinal cord disease
  • Neonatal Encephalopathy: Severe neurological condition
  • Primary Lens Luxation: Eye condition leading to glaucoma
  • …and 190+ more conditions

How MFS Uses Embark Results

  • ✅ No Affected Dogs: We never breed dogs showing two copies of a disease gene
  • ✅ Careful Carrier Pairing: If a dog carries one copy, we only pair with genetically clear partners
  • ✅ Transparency: We share Embark results with Washington families upon request
  • ✅ Ongoing Screening: New dogs added to the program must pass Embark testing

Why Many Breeders Don’t Use Embark

Cost and commitment. Embark testing costs $200+ per dog. For a breeding program with 10-20 dogs, that’s $2,000-$4,000+ investment—before any puppies are even born. Many breeders claim “health testing” but only do basic vet exams or minimal testing. Always ask to see actual Embark reports.
